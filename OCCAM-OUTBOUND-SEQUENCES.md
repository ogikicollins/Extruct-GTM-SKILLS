## COPY RULES
- Under 120 words per email. Under 2 sentences per LinkedIn touch.
- One idea per message. Not two. One.
- Never pitch the service. You're offering a diagnostic.
- Subject lines are 3–5 words. Specific. Not clever.
- End every email with one question or one link. Not both.
- Proof goes in Touch 2. Lead with mechanism or outcome, not a client name.

## REPLY HANDLING

**"Yes, let's do the audit"**
Reply same session. Send Cal.com link. One-line prep note:
> "Bring access to your [Jira / AWS console]. No prep needed beyond that."

**"Not right now — maybe Q3"**
Log the date. CRM reminder in 60 days. Re-enter Touch 2 with:
> "You mentioned Q3 might be better timing. Still true? Audit's still free: [Cal.com link]"

**"We handled this internally"**
Don't accept the close. Ask one question:
> "Good to hear. One follow-up: is there any Atlassian project work coming up — migration, custom development, Data Center — where you'd want outside expertise to support what your team built?"

**"How much does this cost?"**
Never name a price before the audit. Always:
> "The audit itself is free — no cost, no commitment. After the audit, if there's a scope that makes sense, I'll put together a number based on what I actually see. The audit is what makes the number accurate instead of a guess."

**No reply after Touch 4**
Archive. 90-day CRM reactivation. Single re-entry email:
> "[First Name] — it's been a few months. Has anything changed on the [Atlassian/cloud] front at [Company]? Free audit is still available if the timing's better now."
