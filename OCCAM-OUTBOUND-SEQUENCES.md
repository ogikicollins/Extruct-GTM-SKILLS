## THE FRONT-END OFFER ARCHITECTURE

Before the sequences: every single touch in every single playbook leads to one of two offers. These are the only things you are ever asking for. Not a demo. Not a discovery call. An audit.

Audits convert because they deliver real value before money changes hands. The prospect gets something useful whether or not they become a client. That asymmetry removes sales resistance and positions Caleb as a trusted advisor, not a vendor.

---

### OFFER 1: The Atlassian Migration Audit
*Used in Playbooks 1 and 4*

**What it is:**
A 45-minute live screen-share where Caleb personally audits the prospect's Jira and/or Confluence environment.

**What they receive:**
- A **Migration Readiness Score** (1–10) with a written breakdown of exactly where they stand
- Their **top 3 migration risks** — the specific things that will break if not addressed before migration
- An **honest timeline estimate** based on their actual environment (instance size, apps, user count, workflow complexity)
- A **post-migration risk checklist** for the most common failure points
- A **formatted 1-page PDF report** sent the same day

**Value anchor:**
> "We charge $2,500 for this as a standalone paid engagement. I do 4 free per month because it gives me visibility into what IT teams at your stage are actually dealing with."

**Why they can't say no:**
- No commitment, no pitch during the session
- They get a specific, actionable deliverable even if they never hire Occam
- The score and risk report can be used internally to justify budget for a migration project
- 45 minutes is a small ask for a $2,500 value

### OFFER 2: The Cloud Waste Audit
*Used in Playbooks 2 and 3*

**What they receive:**

- The **top 3 cost leak categories** (compute, storage, database, networking, data transfer)
- A **priority fix list** — what to change first to see savings fastest
- A **risk flag** for any security or architecture issues spotted during the review
- A **formatted 1-page report** sent the same day

**Value anchor:**
> "We typically find $3,000–$15,000 per month in recoverable spend during these audits. At 30 minutes of your time, the ROI is immediate regardless of whether we work together."

**Why they can't say no:**
- They get a specific dollar number, not vague advice
- The cost of NOT doing it (continued waste) is quantifiable and ongoing
- 30 minutes is an extremely low ask
- The report gives the CFO or CTO a concrete reason to approve IT spend

### OFFER 3: The IT Rapid Assessment
*Used in Playbook 3 — Trigger Events only*

**What it is:**
A 45-minute structured assessment designed specifically for new technical leaders (new CTO, VP Eng, IT Director) or companies at a major inflection point (post-funding, post-acquisition).

**What they receive:**
- A **current-state map** of their IT stack across three domains: Atlassian, cloud, and DevOps tooling
- A **risk-rated summary** — what's healthy, what's at risk, what's on fire
- A **30-day quick win list** — things that can be improved immediately before any large project begins
- A **priority roadmap** — the 3–5 things to address in the next 6 months
- A **formatted summary document** sent within 24 hours

**Value anchor:**
> "New technical leaders usually spend 30–60 days asking internal teams for status updates and getting optimistic answers. This gives you an independent read in 45 minutes."

**Why they can't say no:**
- Perfectly timed to their situation (new leader, new funding, new complexity)
- Internal teams are incentivized to downplay problems. An external audit is unbiased.
- The deliverable helps them look competent and prepared to their new organization

## SEQUENCE RULES (APPLY TO ALL PLAYBOOKS)

- **Touch 1:** LinkedIn connection request — short, relevant, no pitch
- **Touch 2:** Email — full value offer with the audit, sent 1 day after connection request
- **Touch 3:** LinkedIn message — sent Day 5–6, value drop + reinforce the offer, short
- **Touch 4:** Email — sent Day 12–14, "last message" close, close the loop honestly

**Never pitch the service in Touch 1 or 3.** These touches are for relevance and value delivery only. The ask lives in Touch 2 and Touch 4.

**Reply handling:**
- If they reply "yes, interested" at any touch → stop the sequence, book the audit immediately
- If they reply "not right now" → log the date, re-enter the sequence in 60 days
- If they reply "we don't use Jira / not a fit" → thank them, disqualify, move on

## REPLY HANDLING — WHAT TO DO WHEN THEY RESPOND

**"Yes, I'd love to do the audit" → Immediate:**
Send Cal.com link in the same reply. Confirm time and send a 1-sentence prep note: "All you need to bring is access to your [Jira/AWS] console. No prep needed on your end."

**"Not right now, maybe in Q3" → Nurture:**
Log the date. Set a CRM reminder for 60 days out. Re-enter Touch 2 of the sequence on that date with a simple: "Hi [Name] — you mentioned Q3 might be a better time. Does this week work for the [Audit]?"

**"We just hired someone / we're covered" → Qualify and redirect:**
> "Totally understand — congratulations on the hire. One follow-up question: is there any Atlassian project work (migration, Data Center setup, custom development) coming up where you'd want outside expertise to support your new team? We work alongside internal admins all the time."

**"How much does this cost?" (asked during outreach, not audit):**
Never give a number before the audit. Respond: "The audit itself is free — no cost, no commitment. After the audit, if there's a project scope that makes sense, I'll put together a specific number based on what I see. The audit is what lets me give you an accurate figure rather than a guess."

**No reply after Touch 4 → Archive:**
Move to a "dormant" list in CRM. Set a 90-day re-engagement reminder. Re-enter with a single cold email: "Hi [Name] — it's been a few months since I reached out about [offer]. Has anything changed on the [Atlassian/cloud] front at [Company]? Still happy to run the free [Audit] if it's useful."

## A/B TEST PRIORITY LIST

1. **Subject line test (Touch 2 email)** — test the direct question vs. the offer name vs. the company-specific angle
2. **LinkedIn Touch 1 variant** — test generic vs. signal-specific connection request
3. **Audit name** — test "Atlassian Migration Audit" vs. "Jira Health Check" vs. "Atlassian Risk Assessment"
4. **Touch 3 LinkedIn angle** — test the value drop vs. a specific pain-point story
5. **Touch 4 subject line** — test "Last one" vs. stakes-based subject vs. direct question

Keep whichever version generates more replies over 2–3 weeks of volume. Double down on the winner.
