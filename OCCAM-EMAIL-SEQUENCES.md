## THE RULES

- Email 1: 100–120 words. TOS structure. Proof point. One CTA.
- Email 2: 70–90 words. New angle. One insight. One link.
- Email 3: 60–80 words. Reframe or pattern interrupt. Soft CTA.
- Email 4: 30–50 words. Binary question. Breakup. Nothing else.
- One idea per email. Not two. One.
- Never open with "I hope this finds you well."
- Never say "leverage," "synergy," or "circle back."
- Subject lines: 3–6 words. Lowercase. Specific. Not clever.
- Personalization tokens: `{{first_name}}` `{{company}}` `{{industry}}` `{{role}}` `{{cloud_provider}}`

## REPLY HANDLING

**"Yes, let's do the audit"**
Reply in the same session. Send the Cal.com link. One line:
> "Great — bring access to your [Jira / AWS console]. No prep needed beyond that. See you [day]."

**"Not right now — maybe Q3"**
Log it. CRM reminder in 60 days. Re-enter with Email 1 modified:
> "{{first_name}} — you mentioned Q3 might be better timing. Does this week work for the [Audit]? → [Cal.com link]"

**"We already handled this"**
Don't close. Ask one question:
> "Good to hear. Is there any project work coming up — migration, custom development, Data Center — where you'd want outside expertise alongside the internal team?"

**"What does this cost?"** (asked during outreach)
Never answer before the audit:
> "The audit is completely free — no cost, no commitment. If there's a project scope after it, I'll put together a specific number based on what I actually see. The audit is what makes that number accurate instead of a guess."

**No reply after Email 4**
Archive. 90-day CRM reactivation. Single email:
> "{{first_name}} — it's been a few months. Has anything changed on the [Atlassian/cloud] front at {{company}}? Free audit still available. [Cal.com link]"

## A/B TEST ORDER

Test these one at a time. Change nothing else. Run each test for 2–3 weeks at volume.

1. **Email 1 subject line** — test `{{company}}'s Jira migration` vs `312 projects, 11 days` (curiosity vs. specificity)
2. **Email 2 opener** — test leading with a question vs. leading with an insight
3. **CTA placement** — test link at the end of body vs. mid-email before sign-off
4. **Email 4 length** — test 3-sentence breakup vs. 1-sentence breakup
5. **Proof placement** — test proof statement in Email 1 vs. holding it for Email 2

Keep the winner. Move to the next test.
