# Re-Engagement

Your suppressed list is not a dead list. It is a pre-warmed audience that has already seen your name 5–9 times, has been pre-qualified against your ICP, and — critically — has not said "never." They said "not now." 

When "not now" becomes "now" — triggered by a funding event, a new hire, a competitive signal — the re-engagement sequence converts at 40–60% of your fresh cold outreach rate. With zero list-building cost.

This skill monitors the suppressed list weekly and fires a new sequence the moment a trigger event occurs.

---

## Re-Engagement Trigger Events

A suppressed account re-enters the pipeline **only when a trigger fires.** Do not re-engage on a timer alone (e.g., "it's been 60 days" is not a trigger).

| Trigger | Urgency | Re-engagement angle |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| Series A or B funding announced | CRITICAL (24h) | New budget, new mandate — "congratulations" angle |
| New VP Sales / CRO hired | HIGH (48h) | New leader inheriting broken stack — "first 90 days" angle |
| Active SDR / BDR job post | HIGH (3 days) | Building outbound team — "system before headcount" angle |
| Competitor displacement signal | HIGH (48h) | They're evaluating alternatives — "switching" angle |
| Company LinkedIn post about GTM pain | MEDIUM (5 days) | Self-expressed pain — "saw your post" angle |
| New case study / proof point from SELLL | MEDIUM (1 week) | New relevant proof — "thought you'd want to see this" angle |
| 6 months elapsed + new vertical hypothesis available | LOW | Time-based reset — use only with a new compelling angle |

**Monitor these via `signal-monitor`.** When a trigger is detected for a suppressed account, it automatically gets queued for re-engagement.

## Re-Engagement Sequence Structure

The re-engagement sequence is **shorter than a cold sequence** — 3 emails maximum over 10 days. They know who you are. You don't need 5 emails to introduce yourself.

```
Day 1: Email 1 — The Trigger (reference the new signal, acknowledge the gap)
Day 4: Email 2 — New Proof (something you didn't have last time — new case study or capability)
Day 10: Email 3 — The Clean Breakup (final, no pressure, leave the door open)
```

No LinkedIn touchpoints in re-engagement (they've seen your name enough — don't overdo it). LinkedIn optional only if they have been active recently.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Monitor the suppressed list (weekly via signal-monitor)

The `signal-monitor` skill scans all suppressed accounts as part of its weekly full scan. When a trigger event fires for a suppressed account, it gets added to `claude-code-gtm/engine/re-engagement-queue.md`.

### Step 2: Review the queue

Every Monday, review the re-engagement queue. For each account:
- Confirm the trigger is fresh (within the urgency window)
- Confirm ICP fit is still valid (company hasn't grown out of or below ICP range)
- Confirm no active deal or recent contact exists at the company

### Step 3: Select the re-engagement angle

Match the trigger to the email template above. If multiple triggers fired, use the highest-urgency one.

### Step 4: Generate the 3-email sequence

Personalize each email:
- Reference the specific trigger (funding round size, name of new hire, job post link)
- Use a new proof point not used in the original campaign (from current Proof Library)
- Reference the time gap briefly but don't apologize for it — "we haven't spoken in a while" is enough

### Step 5: Upload as a separate campaign

Create a new campaign in the sequencer: `[Slug] — Re-engagement — [Month]`.

Do NOT put re-engagement sequences in the original campaign. They need separate tracking to measure re-engagement performance independently.

### Step 6: Track outcomes separately

Add to `claude-code-gtm/engine/re-engagement-log.md`:
```
| Company | Original Campaign | Suppressed Date | Trigger | Re-engage Date | Outcome |
```

## Re-Engagement Rules

- **Trigger first, always.** Never re-engage on time alone. If 90 days have passed but nothing new has happened at the company, leave them suppressed until a signal fires.
- **New proof point required.** Every re-engagement sequence must include something you didn't have last time — a new case study, a new outcome, a new capability. If you have nothing new to say, wait.
- **Maximum 3 emails.** They know who you are. If 3 emails with a real trigger don't get a reply, the answer is still "not now." Respect it.
- **Never mention the original campaign.** Don't say "I reached out a few months ago and you didn't reply." Start fresh with the trigger.
- **Separate tracking, always.** Re-engagement conversions must be tracked separately from fresh cold outreach. They inflate your baseline reply rate if mixed together.

## Re-Engagement Metrics

| Metric | Target | Measure |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Trigger detection rate | 100% | Signals caught / signals fired |
| Re-engagement reply rate | 40–60% of fresh cold rate | Re-engage replies / sent |
| Re-engagement → meeting rate | 15–25% | Meetings / re-engaged accounts |
| Time trigger → outreach sent | < 24h for CRITICAL, < 72h for HIGH | From signal detection to send |
| Re-engagement pipeline contribution | 15–25% of total pipeline | Re-engage sourced deals / total |
