# Client Activation Reference — First 30 Days
> Component of: Layer 5 Close + Expand — Phases 1 & 2
> Updated: 2026-06-22

## The 30-Day Activation Principle

The first 30 days of a client engagement determine whether they become a long-term client and referral source, or whether they disengage and churn. Most service providers lose clients in this window because:

1. The "before" state isn't documented (so there's no before/after comparison at Day 90)
2. No quick win is engineered in weeks 1-2
3. Client feels like they're waiting, not building
4. Communication is reactive, not proactive

SELLL's Day 1-30 protocol engineers all four of these deliberately.

## Day-by-Day Activation Calendar

```
DAY 0 — CONTRACT SIGNED
  Aaron:    Review contract, send welcome email (auto-generated by Layer 5)
  n8n:      Create clients.md entry, HubSpot update, Slack celebration
  Client:   Receives welcome email + Day 1-7 plan

DAY 1 — KICKOFF CALL (45 min)
  Aaron:    Runs kickoff call with client (DM + champion if CED triggered in L3)

    1. Restate the goal (their exact pain + the outcome they want)

  Output:
    - Kickoff notes logged in clients.md
    - 90-day plan shared with client as PDF/Notion
    - Access sharing request sent

DAY 3 — ACCESS SHARING DEADLINE
  Client:   Shares access (HubSpot, LinkedIn, sequencer)
  If not received by Day 3:
    n8n: auto-reminder to client (friendly, 1 line)
    If Day 5 and still no access: Aaron personal follow-up
  When access received:
    Aaron: confirms in Slack → n8n starts build sequence

DAY 5 — BRAIN CALIBRATION
  Aaron:    Client brief call (30 min, optional) OR async voice note
  Purpose:  Calibrate tone-DNA to client founder's voice

    - 3 phrases the founder would never use

    - What they hate about agency outbound (what NOT to be)

    - Identify gaps (what they're missing in their ICP right now)

DAY 8-14 — WEEK 2 BUILD

    □ Hypothesis set: customized to client's market

    □ Reviews + approves sequence copy (Day 12 review)

  Day 10 — "Quick Win" milestone:
    n8n: "What did we learn about [Company]'s ICP in week 2?"
    Aaron: sends quick update to client (not waiting for formal weekly)

  Day 12 — Sequence review call (30 min):
    Aaron + client review first 3 sequences together

DAY 14 — MID-BUILD CHECK-IN
  Slack progress update auto-generated (see Layer 5 SKILL.md Step 2B)

    □ Records HeyGen avatar (if opted in — 45 seconds)

    Aaron presents first 10 contacts with scoring rationale
    Client: "yes/no/edit" for each

DAY 21 — WEEK 3 COMPLETE
  All sequences built and approved

DAY 22-28 — WEEK 4: PRE-LAUNCH
  SELLL completes:
    □ Campaign CSV: 20-30 contacts, all 62 columns populated
```

## Before-State Documentation (Day 1 Capture)

This is critical. Without a documented before-state, there's no before/after story at Day 90 — and no case study.

On Day 1 kickoff call, Aaron captures (and logs in clients.md):

```
BEFORE-STATE TEMPLATE
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Current reply rate:       [%] (ask)
Meetings booked/month:    [N] (ask)
SDR count:                [N]
Sequencer:                [tool]
ICP last reviewed:        [date or "never"]
Outbound score out of 10: [client self-rates]
Biggest pain right now:   [verbatim]
Board/CEO expectation:    [what they need to show by when]
What "success" looks like: [verbatim — what they need by Day 90]
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

At Day 90, compare against actual. The delta is the case study.

## Quick Win Engineering (Day 14 Target)

Every client engagement should have a visible quick win by Day 14 — before the campaign even launches. This builds trust for the 90-day journey.

**Day 14 quick win options:**

| Client situation | Quick win to engineer |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| Has existing contact list (but low reply rate) | Re-score their existing list with Layer 2 rubric — show them which contacts are Tier 1 |
| Has sequences but no personalization | Take their best 5 contacts and show them a bespoke AI opener for each |
| Has no ICP clarity | Run ICP workshop + show them which of their past wins share the same buying profile |
| Needs proof to sell internally | Build the ROI calculator output with their own numbers |
| Has a specific account they want to get into | Run Layer 2 on that one account — full intelligence brief |

Quick win delivery note (Day 14, Aaron sends):
```
Subject: [Quick win title] — [Company]

[Name],

Ahead of the full launch: here's what we found when we ran your
[existing list / ICP / target account] through our engine.

[The insight]

This is one example of what the full system does for every contact.
We're on track for the full launch on [Day 28 date].

Aaron
```

## Access Requirements Checklist

```
□ HubSpot:   Read access (review existing contacts, avoid re-contacting clients)
             Admin access preferred (to set up deal properties + pipeline)
□ Instantly: Admin access (set up campaign, domain warmup)
             If not using Instantly: login to current sequencer (Salesloft/Apollo/Lemlist)
□ LinkedIn:  Client's LinkedIn login + Expandi connection OR explicit approval to
             send from client's LinkedIn profile
             ALTERNATIVE: Aaron sends from his own LinkedIn, referencing client
□ Domain:    DNS access or IT contact to set up:
             — SPF/DKIM/DMARC for sending domain
             — CNAME for team.[client-domain] (or approve SELLL's domain)
□ Slack:     Aaron added to client's Slack OR client added to SELLL's Slack
             (for daily updates + reply monitoring)
□ Calendar:  Access to Aaron's calendar link (already live: cal.com/collins-ogiki)
             Client's scheduling tool (Calendly/Cal.com for prospect booking)
□ Content:   Product one-pager or website
             Any existing case studies or client testimonials
             ICP definition doc (if exists — even a rough one)
```

## Client Communication Cadence

```
During Build (Day 1-30):
  Monday:   Written update (auto-generated, Aaron reviews)
  Thursday: Async voice note or Loom (what was built this week — 3 minutes max)
  Weekly:   Check-in call (30 min Tues/Wed/Thurs — NOT Monday)

After Launch (Day 31+):
  Friday:   Automated performance report (Instantly metrics + pipeline)
  Bi-weekly: Check-in call (30 min)
  Monthly:  Business review call (45 min — results + optimization + expansion)
  Quarterly: Strategy session (60 min — next quarter planning)

Response SLA:
  Client email: Aaron responds within 4 hours during business hours
  Client Slack: Aaron responds within 2 hours
  Emergency (system down / deliverability issue): Aaron responds within 30 min
```

## Churn Risk Signals

If any of these occur during Phase 1-2, escalate immediately:

```
🚨 CHURN RISK SIGNALS
1. Client hasn't shared access by Day 5 (engagement dropout)
2. Client cancels two check-in calls in a row without rescheduling
3. Client replies to weekly update with concerns (not questions — concerns)
4. CHS drops below 60 within first 30 days
5. Client asks to "slow things down" or "pause the build"
6. No response from client for 7+ days during build phase

On any signal:
  n8n: Slack alert to Aaron
  Aaron: Personal call (not email, not Slack) within 24 hours
  Goal: identify the real concern before it becomes a cancellation request
```
