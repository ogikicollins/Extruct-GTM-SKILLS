# SELLL.io — Discovery Call Pitch Deck
### Version 1.0 | Built: June 2026 | Format: 9 slides, 18-minute disco

> **How to use this deck:**
> This is a CONVERSATION tool, not a broadcast. Show 2-3 slides, ask a question, listen.
> The slides are designed to make the prospect say "that's exactly us" — then you take over.
> Never read slides verbatim. The speaker notes are what Aaron says while the slide is on screen.
>
> **Deck design:** Dark background (#0D0D0D), white body text, accent color: Electric Blue (#2563EB).
> Use a premium sans-serif (Neue Haas Grotesk, Inter, or Plus Jakarta Sans).
> Each slide = one idea. Maximum 40 words on screen at any time.

# Speaker Notes: Full Disco Script

## Opening (before Slide 1)

> "Before I start — how much context do you want me to give on what we do?
> Some people want the full breakdown, others prefer I ask questions first.
> I'm fine either way."

Most will say "give me the overview." Go to Slide 1.

If they say "ask questions first" — skip the first 4 slides and go straight to Slide 7 (proof),
then Slide 8 (next steps), letting the proof points drive conversation.

---

## Key Discovery Questions (ask at natural breaks)

**After Slide 2:**
"What's your current reply rate? And how many qualified meetings is outbound generating per month?"

**After Slide 3:**
"Do you have a sense of which buying moment — which signal — your best clients were in when they first engaged?"

**After Slide 4:**
"What does your current outbound motion look like? Who owns it, what tools are running, and where's the biggest friction?"

**After Slide 7:**
"Which of those three situations resonates most with where your team is today?"

**After Slide 8:**
"If outbound was producing 8-10 qualified meetings a month — what would that do to your Q4?"

---

## Objection Handling Quick Reference

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|---------|
| "We tried agencies before and they didn't deliver" | "Stefan Golz said the same thing. What specifically failed — was it the list quality, the messaging, or the follow-through?" |
| "This seems expensive" | "Compared to one SDR salary? One SDR is $70K/year. We're $51K for the first year, and the system keeps working after we're done." |
| "We don't have budget right now" | "When does that change? And in the meantime — what's the cost of the current outbound producing [their current meetings/month]?" |
| "We're already using Apollo" | "That's exactly the situation we see most. Apollo is a data tool. The system we build is what tells Apollo who to reach and when. Most Apollo users are running it without the signal layer." |
| "We want to build this in-house" | "That's a 6-12 month build at a minimum. What's the cost of 6 months at current performance while you build? And who's building it?" |
| "Can you send me more information?" | "Absolutely. What specifically do you want to understand better — the process, the pricing, or the results? I'd rather tailor what I send." |
