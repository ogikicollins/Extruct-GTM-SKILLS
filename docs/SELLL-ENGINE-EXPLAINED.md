## What Is SELLL?

SELLL is a fully automated B2B outbound revenue system. It detects companies in an
active buying window, reaches out with precision-personalized outreach, converts positive
replies into booked discovery calls in under 2 hours, manages every deal through to signed
contract, and then turns each closed client into a source of referrals and proof points that
make the next campaign sharper.

It is not a sequence tool. It is not an SDR replacement. It is the entire outbound revenue
motion — from raw market signal to signed contract to client advocacy — operating as one
coordinated system.

**The single metric that defines it:** a traditionally run SDR team generates 35 outreach
activities per day per rep. SELLL runs 200+ personalized conversations per day
automatically, while Aaron's daily time commitment stays at 10 minutes.

---

## The Problem It Solves

Most B2B companies with 25–150 employees and a sales team have the same broken setup:

- **Tools without a system.** They have HubSpot, Apollo, Outreach or Salesloft, and LinkedIn
  Sales Navigator. These tools don't talk to each other, nobody set up the workflows, and
  the sales team manually bridges everything with Slack messages and spreadsheets.

- **Generic outreach getting ignored.** Apollo sequences firing at 0.5–1% reply rates because
  there's no signal layer. The team is sending the right message to the right person at the
  completely wrong time.

- **Unpredictable pipeline.** Strong months, empty months. Over-reliance on one top performer.
  When they leave, pipeline collapses. No repeatable system behind the revenue.

- **SDRs doing the wrong work.** 40–60% of an SDR's time goes to list-building, research, and
  manual follow-up — not selling. A $70K/year SDR is spending $35K worth of their time on tasks
  that can be automated.

SELLL solves all four simultaneously. It builds the system, adds the signal layer,
creates predictable weekly lead flow, and frees the sales team to do what only humans can do:
have the conversation and close the deal.

## Layer 1: Intelligence — The Brain

**What it is:** The knowledge base the entire engine runs on. Everything the engine knows about
who to target, why they buy, what proof converts them, and how to talk to them lives here.

**What it contains:**

| Component | What It Is | Why It Matters |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) | 7-criteria firmographic definition + 7-dimension scoring rubric + 3 detailed personas | Every lead scored against this. No ICP = no targeting discipline. |
| 7 Hypotheses (H1-H7) | The specific buying windows and pain scenarios the engine targets | Each hypothesis maps to a signal, a list query, an email angle, and a proof point |
| 25+ Brain Files | Tone DNA, objection bank, proof library, VOC library, discovery framework, competitive battlecards, ROI calculator, proposal template, vertical playbooks | The engine's institutional memory — everything it knows about how to win |
| 7 Email Sequence Variants | 34 emails across 7 variants, each mapped to a persona + hypothesis combination | Right message for the right persona in the right situation |
| Proof Library | Confirmed client results with before/after numbers and verbatim quotes | Used in Email 2, proposals, and discovery calls to show what's already been built |

**The 7 Hypotheses:**

Each hypothesis is a specific, detectable moment in a company's life where they are most
likely to buy. The engine doesn't send outreach to everyone — it finds companies in one of
these exact windows:

| Code | Name | Signal | Why They Buy Now |
|------|------|--------|-----------------|
| H1 | Post-Raise Pressure | Series A/B raised in last 90 days | New capital + board pipeline review = mandate + budget in the same window |
| H2 | SDR Hiring Trap | Active SDR/BDR job post | Investing in headcount before infrastructure = the exact failure mode we prevent |
| H3 | Founder Ceiling | Founder still closing deals | $5M ARR on relationships; can't scale without a system |
| H4 | Broken Sequence | Apollo + Outreach/Salesloft combo detected | Running sequences at 0.5% reply rate; need a signal layer |
| H5 | New VP Sales Window | VP Sales hired in last 1-45 days | Highest urgency signal. Mandate, budget authority, no political baggage yet. |
| H6 | Lean Rebuild | Post-layoff, rebuilding SDR function | Needs 10x output with same headcount — automation is the only answer |
| H7 | Stuck Audit | Prior GTM consultant engagement detected | Pre-sold on the problem. Has a plan, no execution partner. |

**Compound signals:** When a company matches 2+ hypotheses simultaneously (e.g., H5 + H1 =
new VP Sales at a company that just raised), the Reply Probability formula applies a compound
bonus (+8 for double, +12 for triple). These accounts are the highest-priority in every campaign.

## Layer 2: Activation — Building the Right List

**What it is:** A 5-phase pipeline that takes a raw market signal and produces a scored,
enriched, personalized campaign CSV ready for live outreach. Most agencies skip 80% of this.

**The 5 phases:**

**Phase 0 — Intelligence Gates:** Four hard filters run before any credit is spent. DNC check,
anti-ICP filter (B2C, PLG, enterprise, distressed), re-engagement suppression (no duplicate
outreach to companies contacted in last 90 days), and email bounce estimate. Only companies
that pass all four proceed.

**Phase 1 — Signal Intelligence:** Raw list built from hypothesis-specific queries (LinkedIn
job change alerts, Crunchbase funding data, technographic signals). Compound signal pre-scan
flags accounts matching 2+ hypotheses before enrichment even begins.

**Phase 2 — Account Intelligence:** Full enrichment via Extruct/Prospeo/FullEnrich. Every
company gets: employee count, ARR estimate, funding stage, sequencer tool, CRM, SDR team size,
and a 7-dimension Lead Score (0–100).

**Phase 3 — Contact Intelligence:** Decision maker identified (VP Sales/CRO/Founder). Contact
scored on title seniority, LinkedIn activity, days in role, and reachability. Contact
Intelligence Score (0–50) generated per contact.

**Phase 4 — Outreach Intelligence:** Reply Probability Score calculated for every contact:

```
Reply Probability =
  (Lead Score [0-100] × 0.35)          → max 35 pts
+ (Contact Score [0-50] × 2 × 0.30)   → max 30 pts
+ (Buying Intent [0-10] × 10 × 0.20)  → max 20 pts
+ (Warm Path Bonus [0-100] × 0.10)    → max 10 pts
+ (Urgency Window [0-100] × 0.05)     → max  5 pts
+ (Compound Signal Bonus)              → +8 or +12
```

Contacts sorted into three tiers: Tier 1 Priority (Reply Prob ≥ 70), Tier 1 Standard
(50-69), Tier 2 (35-49). Tier 1 Priority gets bespoke AI video, personalized subject lines,
and Thread C multi-stakeholder outreach. Tier 2 gets standard sequence personalization.

**Phase 5 — Output:** 62-column campaign CSV with every merge variable populated, sequence
variants assigned, pre-engagement schedule built (T-3/T-2/T-1 LinkedIn warm-up), and call
queue created for contacts with phone numbers.

## Layer 3: Campaign Execution — The 9 Superpowers

**What it is:** The living campaign intelligence system. Layer 3 doesn't just send emails on
a timer — it watches every interaction in real time, adapts what each contact receives based
on how they've engaged, and converts HOT replies into booked meetings in under 2 hours.

**Aaron's daily time while Layer 3 runs: 10 minutes.**

### The 9 Superpowers

These are capabilities no off-the-shelf tool replicates. They are the engine's moat.

---

**Superpower 1: Behavioral Intent Score (BIS)**

Every contact gets a BIS (0-100) that updates in real time on every interaction:

| Event | BIS Change | What It Means |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| Email opened within 1h | +8 | High relevance — opened immediately |
| Email opened 3-4 times | +15 | Returning to read — high intent |
| Email opened 5+ times | +20 | Ghost Positive protocol triggers |
| Link clicked | +20 | Direct engagement with content |
| HeyGen video clicked | +25 | Strongest pre-reply intent signal |
| Email forwarded | +30 | Buying committee forming |
| LinkedIn profile visited | +15 | Research mode — active evaluation |
| Expandi connection accepted | +10 | Warm signal |
| Expandi DM replied | +20 | Dialogue started |

Most tools score a contact once at list-build time and never update it. The BIS updates on
every event. A contact who opened Email 1 twice and clicked the video is now scored 91 — Aaron
knows to treat them as near-HOT before they've replied.

---

**Superpower 2: Compound Engagement Detector (CED)**

When two or more contacts at the same company both cross BIS 50, the CED fires an account-level
buying signal. No off-the-shelf tool tracks engagement across contacts — they track individuals.

CED trigger: "Two people at Luminary Health are independently researching. This is a buying
committee forming, not a coincidence."

Action: Thread C auto-arms (economic buyer outreach), priority escalates, Aaron receives a
multi-stakeholder brief.

---

**Superpower 3: Sequence Adaptation Engine (SAE)**

Email 3, 4, and 5 are NOT the same for every contact. Before each email queues, the SAE
evaluates how the contact engaged with prior emails and selects the optimal variant:

- BIS ≥ 70: AI-personalized email with HeyGen video
- BIS 50-69 + link clicked: Proof-heavy variant (double down on case study)
- BIS 30-49 + opened 3x: Insight variant (challenger angle, no social proof)
- No opens at all: Subject line swap from spintax pool

Most outbound tools send the same Email 3 to everyone. SAE sends the right Email 3 to each
person based on how they've engaged with the first two.

---

**Superpower 4: LinkedIn Engagement Mirror (LEM)**

When a contact engages with Aaron's LinkedIn content during an active campaign — even a like
on a post — Expandi fires a warm DM convergence within 4 hours:

"Hey [Name] — thanks for engaging with the post. That's exactly the situation I referenced
in the email I sent you. Worth a quick conversation?"

Two independent signals converging (email + LinkedIn) in the same 48-hour window reads as
synchronous, not automated. This is a genuine conversion differentiator.

---

**Superpower 5: Auto-Discovery Brief (ADB)**

The moment a HOT reply lands, Claude API generates a complete 1-page discovery brief
and delivers it to Aaron's Slack in 62 seconds:

1. Situation summary (what we know about this company and contact)
2. Why they replied (what in the email likely landed)
3. Top 3 discovery questions tailored to their role and hypothesis
4. Most likely objection + counter from the objection bank
5. Recommended proof point matched to their situation
6. ROI estimate using their exact numbers
7. Suggested next step

Aaron reads it in 2 minutes. He walks into every call more prepared than most founders are
after 20 minutes of manual research.

---

**Superpower 6: Speed-to-Book Auto-Assist**

Industry benchmark: responding to a HOT reply within 2 hours produces an 85% show rate.
Responding within 24 hours produces a 40% show rate. Every hour of delay costs conversions.

The system enforces a 2-hour SLA automatically:
- T+0: HOT reply arrives, ADB delivered to Slack in 62 seconds
- T+60min: If Aaron hasn't responded, a holding email auto-sends: "Flagged this — give me until [2h]. Want to give you a proper answer."
- T+2h: Escalation alert. Aaron is notified urgently.
- MEETING_REQUEST: Calendar link auto-sends in 60 seconds. No human required.

---

**Superpower 7: Ghost Positive Video Re-activation**

A contact who opens the same email 5+ times over multiple days is showing clear buying intent
without replying. Most systems ignore this. SELLL fires a new personalized HeyGen video
as an out-of-sequence touch:

"Hey [Name] — noticed you came back to this a few times. Whatever caught your attention —
I wanted to follow up directly. [Personalized reference to their company + proof point].
If something in there was relevant: [calendar link]."

The video arrives the next business morning, out of sequence, as a direct acknowledgment.
The effect: a contact who was curious but not ready to reply often converts here.

---

**Superpower 8: Competitor Displacement Injection**

When a target company posts about frustration with a competitor during an active campaign,
the injection fires within 24 hours:

Detection: LinkedIn Sales Navigator "New Mentions" alerts for competitor names (Belkins, CIENCE,
Apollo, Outreach, Salesloft, Lemlist) filtered to active campaign accounts. Aaron confirms
(1 click), then the injection fires automatically.

What happens: the next scheduled sequence email is replaced with a displacement email
referencing their exact post, the specific competitor, and the most relevant proof point
from the competitive battlecard. Sequence resumes normally afterward.

The contact receives an email that proves we were listening — at the exact moment their
frustration with the alternative is highest.

---

**Superpower 9: Auto-Proposal Pre-generation**

After a qualified discovery call (score ≥ 18/25), Claude API assembles a full draft proposal
before Aaron's next meeting:

- Their exact pain in their own words (from call notes)
- 90-day build plan with specific deliverables
- ROI calculation using their SDR count, current reply rate, and ACV
- Proof point matched to their vertical and persona
- Investment section with payback timeline

Draft delivered to Slack within minutes of logging the call. Aaron edits and sends within 24
hours. Proposals sent within 24h of a discovery call close at 3x the rate of proposals sent
3+ days later.

## Layer 4: Pipeline Intelligence — Deals Never Stall Silently

**What it is:** Full deal lifecycle management from first HOT reply to signed contract.
Every deal has a Deal Health Score (DHS) updated daily. The pipeline never goes cold
without Aaron being alerted.

**Deal Health Score (DHS) — 0 to 100:**

| Dimension | Max | Scoring |
|-----------|-----|---------|
| Recency of engagement | 25 | Last touch < 3 days = 25; 7+ days = 5; 14+ days = 0 |
| Stage velocity | 20 | Moving forward = 20; stalled 10d+ = 0 |
| Decision maker confirmed | 15 | DM + authority verified = 15; unknown = 0 |
| Budget signal | 15 | Budget confirmed = 15; unknown = 0 |
| Champion engaged | 10 | Champion replied this week = 10; none = 0 |
| Proposal opened 2+ times | 10 | 2+ views = 10; not yet = 0 |
| Competitive threat | 5 | None known = 5; active competitor named = 0 |

DHS thresholds: 80+ = GREEN (on track), 60-79 = AMBER (needs attention),
40-59 = RED (stall risk — call and escalate), < 40 = CRITICAL (at risk).

**What happens automatically:**
- Daily 06:30 UTC: Pipeline dashboard delivered to Slack with all deal DHS scores and today's action list
- Day 7 no touch: Stall alert fires with specific action recommendation
- Day 10 no touch: Deal stage moves to "Stalled", re-engagement sequence triggers
- Discovery score ≥ 18: Proposal auto-generated and delivered for Aaron's review
- Proposal opened 2+ times: Slack alert — "accelerate close sequence now"
- Contract signed: Layer 5 onboarding triggers automatically

**Aaron's daily time on pipeline: 15 minutes.**

**What it is:** The system that makes every layer smarter over time. Without Layer 6, the
engine delivers Month 1 performance indefinitely. With it, Month 6 outperforms Month 1 by

### The 8 Feedback Loops

| Loop | Data Source | What Updates | Downstream Effect |
|------|------------|-------------|------------------|
| 1. ICP Tightening | Layer 2-3 scores vs. actual HOT converters | IDEAL-CUSTOMER-PROFILE.md scoring weights | Next list has higher signal density |
| 2. Sequence Optimization | Open/reply rates by email number and subject | Active sequence variants + spintax pool | Week-over-week copy improvement |
| 3. Proof Loop | Discovery call notes + client results | proof-library.md | Email 2 and proposals convert better |
| 4. Objection Learning | Sales call objections | objection-bank.md | Close sequences pre-empt resistance |
| 5. VOC Library | Exact buyer phrases from HOT replies | voc-library.md + Email 2/3 copy | Emails mirror how buyers talk |
| 6. Win Lookalike | Won client profiles | Layer 2 list-build seed | Next lists look more like clients |
| 7. Call Timing | Connect rates by time of day | cold-call skill call windows | Higher connect rates over time |
| 8. Hypothesis Performance | Reply rates by hypothesis per campaign | hypothesis_set.md + Layer 2 scoring | Best signals get more volume; weak ones retire |

### The Compounding Effect

```
Month 1:  H5 campaign — 2.1% reply rate
          ↓ Layer 6 learns: Day 22-31 contacts underperform Day 1-15 by 40%
          ↓ Layer 1 update: H5 scoring adjusted (Day 31-45 deprioritized)

Month 3:  H5 optimized — 4.2% reply rate (+100%)

```
