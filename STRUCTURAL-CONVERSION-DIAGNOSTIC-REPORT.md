# Structural Chat — Conversion Diagnostic
Prepared for Paul Chiusano · 2026-07-17 · Source: full call-log export, 112 calls, 7/10–7/17, all reps

This answers the four questions raised for the weekly review: what's working/not working, the objection pattern behind non-converting calls, transcript review, and email status.

---

## 1. The funnel, with real numbers

Of 112 logged dials this period, 105 were genuine Structural Chat attempts (7 were a data-tagging issue on our side, unrelated to targeting or script — see note at the bottom).

| Outcome | Count | % of 105 |
|---|---|---|
| Wrong number | 31 | 30% |
| Busy | 17 | 16% |
| DNC | 10 | 10% |
| Left live message | 4 | 4% |
| **Connected (real conversation, no meeting)** | 39 | 37% |
| **Meeting booked** | 4 | 4% |

**The single biggest lever isn't objection handling — it's reachability.** 55% of dials (58/105) never became a conversation at all: wrong numbers, busy signals, DNC. That's a list/data-quality problem, not a messaging problem, and it's the ceiling on everything downstream.

**Of the calls that did connect, the real conversion rate is 9.3%** (4 meetings / 43 conversations, counting both "Connected" and "Meeting Booked" as reaching a live conversation) — not the 2.6% figure previously in circulation. We traced that number precisely: it was 78 total dial attempts through 7/14, of every kind — 23 wrong numbers and 14 busy signals were counted as "conversations" alongside the 32 real ones. Once you count only calls where someone actually answered and talked, roughly 1 in 11 books, not 1 in 38.

## 2. The objection pattern (the question that's been outstanding the longest)

Of the 39 connected-but-not-booked calls, 31 have a usable call summary (8 had no notes captured). Here's the real distribution:

| Reason | Share | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| **Already has something in place** | **39% (12/31)** | The dominant pattern. A mix of "we built it ourselves" (in-house engineering, agent-background hires) and "we're on Zendesk/Intercom/HubSpot already." |
| Wrong contact reached | 26% (8/31) | Sales reps, engineers, recently-departed staff — not the support/CX owner. |
| Timing | 13% (4/31) | Mid-transition, traveling, post-acquisition — asked to reconnect later, not a no. |
| Too small / low volume | 10% (3/31) | Genuinely below the volume threshold where automation pays off. |
| Still warm | 10% (3/31) | Not a loss — includes Parspec, which converted to one of the 4 booked meetings, and Max Retail / Municipal District Services, both active follow-ups. |

**"Already has something in place" being the largest single reason, by a wide margin, matches exactly what you flagged in writing before calls even started** — the displacement-vs-defended split. That framework is now built into the live call script: reps ask whether cases are actually all getting handled by the existing bot, and only push the upgrade angle when the answer is no. Worth watching next week's data to see if that shifts this number.

**Wrong contact at 26% is a targeting fix, not a script fix** — tightening title filters on list pulls should move this directly.
