# ALIF Agency — GTM Strategy
> Built by SELLL.io | Head of Sales | 2026-06-28
> Objective: 35%+ close rate on qualified discovery calls within 90 days
> Context: Full strategy for ALIF Agency (alifagency.ai) — Dubai creative + digital agency, 6-person team, targeting brands in MENA/EU/US

---

## Executive Summary

ALIF Agency has the delivery proof ($2M+ client revenue, 98% satisfaction, 100+ projects) and the positioning edge (creative + performance unified). What they lack is a sales engine. They are closing roughly 10–15% of conversations today — mostly from inbound and referrals — and they have no systematic outbound motion.

This strategy builds the system to reach 35%+ conversion. The path is not volume — it's precision. Sharpen the ICP. Reach the right companies at the right moment. Lead with value before asking for anything. Convert with a discovery framework built for ALIF's specific buyer, not a generic agency pitch.

**35% is achievable because:**
1. ALIF's integrated offering (creative + performance) solves a real, documented pain — brands that have fragmented vendors with disconnected outputs
2. The free Creative & Performance Audit creates pre-sold prospects — they've already seen the gap before the proposal
3. ALIF has documented proof ($2M+ revenue generated) that most Dubai agencies cannot match
4. The Dubai/MENA market is relationship-driven — speed to relationship + WhatsApp follow-up beats email-heavy funnels

## Part 1 — Sharpened ICP

### The Problem With ALIF's Current Positioning

"Established brands and startups across MENA, Europe, and North America" = no ICP. Targeting everyone means the outreach reads as generic, discovery takes too long to find fit, and conversion suffers because ALIF can't reference a specific "company just like yours" when proposing.

The sharpened ICP focuses on three segments where ALIF's integrated model wins fastest and converts at 35%+.

---

### Primary ICP: Growth-Stage D2C & E-Commerce Brands (MENA)

**Who they are:** UAE/KSA/Egypt-based consumer brands — fashion, beauty, food, lifestyle — that are doing $500K–$5M in annual sales and are ready to invest in brand building + performance scaling simultaneously.

**Why they convert:** They have a product that works, budget to invest ($5K–$15K/month), and a founder who understands that the next stage of growth requires both brand equity AND performance. They feel the pain of having a creative agency that doesn't understand ROAS, or a performance agency that makes their brand look cheap.

**Firmographic signals:**
- 10–100 employees
- Active on Instagram + TikTok (already investing in content)
- Running Meta or Google ads (even if poorly)
- $500K–$5M in tracked online revenue (Shopify, Noon, Amazon UAE presence)
- Based in UAE, KSA, or Egypt

**Buying triggers:**
- Launching a new product line or category
- Entering a new GCC market (e.g., KSA-based brand expanding to UAE)
- Post-funding (angel or seed round)
- Running ads but ROAS below 2x (pain is documented)
- Just fired previous agency

**Decision maker:** Founder/CEO or Head of Marketing (in role <18 months)

**Average deal size:** AED 15,000–40,000/month retainer ($4K–$11K USD)

**Conversion rate potential with ALIF's model:** 38–45% (highest converting segment)

---

### Secondary ICP: Hospitality, F&B & Lifestyle Brands (Dubai)

**Who they are:** Dubai restaurants, hotel lifestyle brands, experience businesses, luxury retail — that need stunning visual content + strong organic/paid social + a website that converts.

**Why they convert:** Dubai hospitality is image-driven. A bad Instagram or a slow website loses customers immediately. These owners understand that marketing is mandatory, not optional. They pay for quality and they refer aggressively within the Dubai hospitality community.

**Firmographic signals:**
- Single-location or small chain (2–5 locations) in Dubai
- 15–80 employees
- Has a physical location but weak digital presence
- Posting on social inconsistently
- Website is outdated or absent

**Buying triggers:**
- New location opening
- Rebranding (name change, concept change)
- Slow season and owner wants to drive traffic
- Just returned from a trip to Europe/NY and wants their brand to feel world-class

**Decision maker:** Owner/GM directly (not a marketing manager)

**Average deal size:** AED 10,000–25,000/month ($2.7K–$6.8K USD)

**Conversion rate potential with ALIF's model:** 32–40%

---

### Tertiary ICP: Tech Startups & Scale-Ups (MENA/EU Crossover)

**Who they are:** Seed-to-Series A companies that raised capital and now need to build brand presence alongside performance. Often have a founder who is technical, understands the product deeply, but hasn't invested in marketing design or content systems.

**Why they convert:** They have budget (post-raise), urgency (investors want traction), and a clear need for both brand credibility AND lead generation — exactly ALIF's unified offering.

**Firmographic signals:**
- $500K–$5M raise in last 12 months
- 15–100 employees
- HQ in Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Riyadh, or major EU city (London, Berlin, Amsterdam)
- Has a website but poor brand design
- Running ads but no clear creative strategy

**Buying triggers:**
- Funding announced
- New CMO or Head of Marketing hired
- Launching in new market
- Preparing for Series A/B fundraise (needs investor-grade brand)

**Decision maker:** Founder/CEO + CMO/Head of Marketing

**Average deal size:** AED 20,000–60,000/month ($5.4K–$16K USD)

**Conversion rate potential with ALIF's model:** 28–35%

## Part 2 — The 35% Conversion System

Standard agency conversion is 10–15%. The gap between 15% and 35% is closed by five specific mechanics.

---

### Mechanic 1: The Free Creative & Performance Audit

**What it is:** Before any sales conversation, ALIF runs a 20-minute audit of the prospect's brand — website, social, ads (Facebook Ad Library), SEO, and visual identity. The audit surfaces 3–5 specific, named gaps with visual examples.

**Why it converts:** The prospect sees their problem before ALIF asks for anything. They walk into the discovery call pre-sold on the pain. The proposal becomes the logical answer to gaps they've already acknowledged.

**How to deliver:**
1. Use the audit as the reason for initial outreach: "I ran a quick audit of [Brand]'s digital presence. Found 3 things worth sharing — can I send them over?"
2. Deliver audit as a brief PDF or Loom video (5 minutes max) — visual, specific, named gaps
3. Book discovery call to walk through gaps together

**Conversion impact:** Prospects who receive an audit before a discovery call convert at 2–3x the rate of prospects who get a cold pitch.

---

### Mechanic 2: Signal-Triggered Outreach Only

Only reach out when a real buying signal has fired. No spray-and-pray.

| Signal | Source | Action |
|--------|--------|--------|
| New funding announced | LinkedIn, Crunchbase, Gulf News | Outreach within 24 hours |
| New CMO/Head of Marketing hired | LinkedIn "Changed Jobs" filter | Outreach within 48 hours (VP Sales persona) |
| New location or product launch posted | Instagram, company website | "Saw your launch" angle |
| Running bad ads (visible in Facebook Ad Library) | FB Ad Library | Audit angle — specific creative critique |
| New website launched (Builtwith change) | Builtwith/Wappalyzer alerts | "Saw your new site" angle |
| Posting about marketing frustrations | LinkedIn, Instagram DM | Reference their own words |
| Just fired previous agency (job post: "looking for agency") | LinkedIn Jobs | Displacement angle |

---

### Mechanic 3: Three-Tier Proposal — Remove Price as the Barrier

The most common reason proposals stall: the prospect wants what ALIF offers but has sticker shock or uncertainty about commitment.

**The Three-Tier Proposal Model:**

| Tier | Name | Price Range | Includes | Commitment |
|------|------|-------------|----------|------------|
| Quick Win | Brand Sprint | AED 8,000–15,000 one-time | Brand audit + identity refresh + 1 month social strategy | Project-based, no commitment |
| Core Partner | Growth Retainer | AED 15,000–30,000/month | Social + Content + Paid Ads + Performance Reporting | 3-month minimum |
| Full System | Brand × Performance Partner | AED 35,000–60,000/month | Everything: brand, web, content, ads, production, monthly strategy | 6-month engagement |

**Why three tiers work:** Prospects who "can't commit" to the Full System still say yes to the Brand Sprint — and 70%+ of Brand Sprint clients convert to the Growth Retainer within 60 days after seeing results.

---

### Mechanic 4: WhatsApp-First Follow-Up

Dubai market reality: email open rates average 18–22%. WhatsApp message open rates average 95%+. Every proposal sent via email must be followed up via WhatsApp within 2 hours.

**WhatsApp follow-up protocol:**
- Day 0 (proposal sent): WhatsApp — "Hey [Name] — sent the proposal to your email. Happy to walk through the Brand Sprint option in 15 minutes if easier. Just say the word."
- Day 2 (no reply): WhatsApp voice note from Kaya — 45 seconds, personal, referencing something specific to their brand
- Day 5 (no reply): WhatsApp — "Saw [something about their brand/market] this week that reminded me of the gap we talked about. Is timing an issue or do you have questions on the proposal?"
- Day 10 (no reply): Final WhatsApp — "Last one from me — if now isn't right, no issue at all. I'll share a case study from [similar brand] in case it's useful later."

---

### Mechanic 5: Active Referral Engine

ALIF has 98% client satisfaction and 100+ projects delivered. That is an untapped referral asset. Most agencies leave referrals to chance. SELLL builds a systematic referral ask into every client engagement.

**Protocol:**
- Day 30 of every new retainer: "Who in your network would benefit from what we're doing for you?"
- End of every project: Formal referral ask with a clear process ("If you introduce us to one brand, we give you one free month of social content")
- LinkedIn: Tag client in milestone posts (with permission) — creates social proof + passive referral
- Testimonial → case study pipeline: Every happy client becomes a case study; every case study becomes an outreach proof point

**Referral conversion rate:** 55–70% (vs. 12% for cold outreach). Building the referral engine is the fastest path to 35%+ overall close rate.

## Part 7 — Objection Handling (ALIF-Specific)

### "We've worked with agencies before and they didn't deliver"
> "That's exactly why we start with an audit, not a pitch. Before we talk scope or price, I want to show you three specific gaps I found in your current [website/social/ads] — and what fixing them is worth in real revenue. If the audit doesn't show you something valuable, there's no reason to go further. Can I send it over?"

### "We're doing it in-house"
> "Most teams that are doing it in-house are great at one part — either the creative or the ads. What we consistently find is the disconnect between the two is where revenue leaks. Our best clients came to us running ads that didn't match their brand — their cost-per-lead was 3x higher than it needed to be. Worth taking a look at where you are on that?"

### "Your pricing is too high"
> "Totally fair to raise that. Before I talk numbers, help me understand what success looks like for you. If we generate [X] qualified leads per month at [Y] cost, what does that mean in revenue terms? [Listen.] Most clients find that the retainer pays back within 60 days when we tie it to the right outcomes. What's your current cost to acquire a customer?"

### "We need to think about it"
> "Of course — what specifically are you thinking through? If it's about timing, we can structure a Brand Sprint first — one project, no commitment, you see the output. If it's about budget, let's look at the mid-tier option. If it's about trust, I'd rather you spoke to one of our current clients directly than wait alone with a proposal."

### "We want to focus on [one channel] only"
> "Totally your call. Our experience in the UAE market is that single-channel campaigns plateau faster because there's no creative reinforcement across touchpoints. But I'm not going to push you into something you don't want. Can I show you the audit first? If the single-channel option is the right call after you've seen the gaps, we can structure it that way."

### "We're already talking to another agency"
> "Good — you should compare. What are the two or three things that matter most to you in that comparison? I'd rather help you make the right call than win a pitch by saying the right things. [Listen.] Here's one thing most agencies in Dubai can't show you: [reference specific case study with real numbers]. That's the bar we'd hold ourselves to."
