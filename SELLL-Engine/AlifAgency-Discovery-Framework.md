## Framework Overview

ALIF's discovery call is not a pitch. It is a diagnostic. The prospect already received the audit — they came in knowing there's a gap. The call's job is to:

1. Confirm the pain is real and specific
2. Understand what success looks like (so the proposal matches exactly)
3. Uncover budget and decision authority
4. Set the emotional context for the proposal to land

**Time:** 25–30 minutes total
**Format:** Video call (Zoom/Meet) or in-person (preferred for Dubai clients)
**Who attends from ALIF:** Kaya Geha + optionally Amir Nadiri if the conversation is performance-heavy

**Opening line (say this verbatim at the top of every call):**
> "I've got 25 minutes blocked. Here's what I want to cover: first, I want to understand what's actually happening with your marketing right now — the real situation, not the polished version. Then I'll share what the audit found and what we'd do differently. At the end we'll figure out together if it makes sense to go deeper. Sound good?"

## Pre-Call Brief (Kaya's Prep Checklist)

Before every call, spend 10 minutes on this:

- [ ] Re-read the prospect's website, Instagram, and LinkedIn in the last 24 hours
- [ ] Check their Facebook Ad Library — what are they running right now?
- [ ] Note one thing that changed since the last time you looked at their brand
- [ ] Identify which of ALIF's three proof points is most relevant to their vertical
- [ ] Know their approximate revenue range (use Crunchbase, LinkedIn headcount, or stated funding)
- [ ] Have their audit doc open on a second screen
- [ ] Know their decision timeline (does the trigger suggest urgency — new location? post-raise?)
- [ ] Prepare the specific ALIF case study most relevant to their industry

## Discovery Questions (By Dimension)

### Dimension 1: Current Situation (5 minutes)

**Q1: The State of Play**
> "Walk me through your marketing right now — what channels, what's working, what isn't."

*Listen for:* Fragmented channels, spending on ads without clear ROAS, social that looks beautiful but doesn't convert, inconsistency in visual brand across touchpoints.
*Green flag:* "We're spending on ads but our agency does ads and our other agency does the brand and they don't talk to each other."
*Red flag:* "Everything is actually working well — I'm just curious about what you do." → Probe for the real pain before going further.

---

**Q2: The Budget Signal**
> "What are you currently spending on marketing — ads, agency fees, content creation, all in?"

*Listen for:* Anything above AED 10,000/month signals they have budget and are already invested in marketing.
*Green flag:* "About AED 20–30K/month" → Clear budget for ALIF's core packages.
*Yellow flag:* "We're spending AED 5K on ads but nothing else" → Could be right for Brand Sprint.
*Red flag:* "We haven't spent anything yet" → Too early for retainer; may be right for one-time project.

---

**Q3: The Result Question**
> "What does that spending produce right now — in real outcomes? Leads, reservations, sales, whatever the right metric is for you."

*Listen for:* Inability to answer this clearly = they're not tracking properly = ALIF's analytics layer is a strong add.
*Green flag:* "Honestly I don't know — that's part of the problem." → Pre-sold on needing a system.
*Red flag:* "We're getting great ROAS — 4x return on ad spend." → Probe what's broken before pitching.

---

**Q4: The History Question**
> "Have you worked with a marketing agency before? What happened?"

*Listen for:* Prior agency failures are common in Dubai — and they make ALIF's integrated model more compelling.
*Green flag:* "We tried two agencies — one did brand, one did ads, they didn't coordinate and we ended up with a beautiful brand that nobody saw."
*Gold flag:* "We paid AED 15K/month and got zero reporting — I didn't even know what they were doing."
*Red flag:* "We had a great experience and just need to expand scope." → Understand why they're looking again before assuming they want to switch.

---

### Dimension 2: Goals and Success Definition (5 minutes)

**Q5: The 90-Day Success Question**
> "If our work together delivered exactly what you hoped — what would be different 90 days from now?"

*Listen for:* Specific, measurable outcomes. Vague = they haven't defined success = harder to close.
*Green flag:* "I want 30 qualified inbound inquiries per month" or "I want to double my Instagram following and drive 20% more restaurant covers."
*Probe if vague:* "When you say 'more visibility' — what does that look like in real numbers for [Brand]?"

---

**Q6: The Priority Question**
> "On a scale of 1–10, how important is solving your marketing gap in the next 60 days versus everything else you're managing?"

*Green flag:* 7+ with a specific reason ("we open the new location in 8 weeks").
*Yellow flag:* 5–6 → Ask: "What would make this a 9?"
*Red flag:* Below 5 → Low urgency; de-prioritize. Add to nurture.

---

**Q7: The Alternative Question**
> "What happens if you don't fix this before [trigger event]? Is there a plan B?"

*Listen for:* No plan B = urgency. Clear plan B = lower urgency.
*Green flag:* "There isn't one — we need to make this season work."
*Red flag:* "We could try to hire someone in-house." → Address the hire-vs-agency trade-off directly.

---

### Dimension 3: Budget and Authority (5 minutes)

**Q8: The Decision Question**
> "Is this a decision you can make, or does it involve someone else?"

*Green flag:* "I own the company — it's my call." → Proceed to close.
*Yellow flag:* "I'd need to check with my business partner." → Ask to include them in the next call.
*Red flag:* "It goes to a committee." → Adjust timeline expectations; get a champion.

---

**Q9: The Investment Framing Question**
> "When you think about marketing spend, are you thinking about it as a fixed monthly expense or as an investment with a target return?"

*Listen for:* ROI-minded buyers are easier to close on the Growth Retainer (higher ACV, better LTV). Cost-minded buyers need the Brand Sprint first.
*Green flag:* "Investment — I want to see the math."
*Cost-minded response:* "Let me show you what the return looks like on a AED 15,000/month retainer for a [brand like yours]."

---

**Q10: The Timeline Question**
> "When would you want to start — is there a deadline driving this?"

*Green flag:* "We open in 8 weeks" or "Our new product launches in 6 weeks." → Specific deadline = urgency.
*Red flag:* "No rush, whenever." → Low urgency; may not close quickly. Pitch Brand Sprint as low-commitment first step.

---

### Dimension 4: Fit and Positioning (5 minutes)

**Q11: The Competitive Question**
> "Are you looking at other agencies as well, or is this an exploratory conversation?"

*Green flag:* "We're comparing 2–3 options." → Ask what matters most in the comparison. Show ALIF's integrated advantage.
*Red flag:* "We're talking to 6 agencies." → They're collecting proposals, not making a decision. Accelerate with a quick-win proposal and WhatsApp follow-up.

**Response when comparing:**
> "Good — you should. What matters most to you in that comparison — creative quality, performance results, or how fast they can deliver? [Listen.] Here's what most of our clients tell us after comparing: most agencies are great at one thing. We run creative and performance from the same strategy. That's the gap that usually makes the difference."

---

**Q12: The Team Question**
> "Who on your team would be working with us day-to-day? Is it you, or is there a marketing person we'd interface with?"

*Listen for:* Identify the day-to-day champion. If there's no marketing person = ALIF becomes their marketing team = higher ACV potential.

## The Audit Walk-Through (Mid-Call, 5–8 minutes)

After questions 1–7, present the audit findings. This is the turning point in the call.

**How to present the audit:**
1. Share screen — show their actual brand (website, Instagram, ads)
2. Present 3 specific findings — not generic advice, specific observations about THEIR brand
3. For each finding: State what you see → What it costs them → What fixing it looks like
4. Don't prescribe the solution yet — let them feel the gap first

**Framework per finding:**
> "What I see: [specific observation]. What that means for you: [specific impact on revenue/results]. What fixing it looks like: [one sentence — save the detail for the proposal]."

**Example for a Dubai F&B brand:**
> "What I see: Your Instagram posts in the last 30 days have four different visual styles — different filters, different text placement, different color temperature. What that costs you: it makes [Brand] feel like four different restaurants to someone scrolling their feed for the first time. What fixing it looks like: a unified visual identity system where every post, story, and ad looks unmistakably like [Brand]."

## The Close Setup (Final 5 minutes)

**Q13 — The Alignment Question:**
> "Based on what you've heard — do you see your situation in what I've described? Is this the right direction?"

*Listen for:* "Yes" = proceed to proposal. "Kind of" = probe the hesitation. "No" = re-qualify.

---

**Q14 — The Blocker Question:**
> "What would need to be true for you to feel confident moving forward — what's the one thing that would make this a clear yes?"

*This surfaces the real objection before it becomes a stall in the proposal stage.*

*Common answers and responses:*
- "I need to see more case studies from my industry" → "I'll include two specific examples in the proposal by tomorrow."
- "I need to get approval from my partner" → "Can we do a 20-minute call with both of you this week? I'll make the proposal easy for that conversation."
- "The budget is tight" → "Let me build the Brand Sprint option into the proposal — it's the lowest-commitment entry point with the fastest ROI."
- "I just need to think about it" → "I hear that — what specifically are you thinking through? If there's a concern I haven't addressed, I'd rather handle it now than have it hang over the proposal."

---

**Q15 — The Commitment Test:**
> "If the proposal looks right, what does the path to a decision look like on your side — and when can we have it done?"

*Get a specific date. No date = no urgency.*
*Green flag:* "If I like the proposal, I can sign off within the week."
*Yellow flag:* "I need to show my business partner." → Offer to present the proposal together on a call.

## Post-Call Protocol (2 Hours)

**Step 1 (0–15 min):** Log qualification score and key notes in KommoCRM. Flag the lead stage.

**Step 2 (within 1 hour):** Send the post-call WhatsApp:
> "Great talking [First Name] — sending the proposal tomorrow morning. One thing I'll make sure to include: [specific thing they mentioned matters most]. Anything else to add before I build it?"

**Step 3 (within 24 hours):** Send the proposal — Three-Tier structure, AED-denominated, mobile-friendly PDF. Include 2 case studies matched to their industry.

**Step 4 (within 2 hours of proposal sending):** WhatsApp follow-up:
> "[First Name] — proposal is in your inbox. Happy to walk through it together in 15 minutes whenever suits you this week."

**Step 5 (if no reply in 48 hours):** WhatsApp voice note from Kaya — personal, 30 seconds, reference one specific thing from the call.

## Proposal Structure (Three-Tier Template)

Every proposal from ALIF follows this structure. Mobile-friendly. One page per tier.

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Cover** | [Brand]'s name + ALIF logo + "Marketing Partnership Proposal — [Date]" |
| **The Gap** | 3 audit findings in plain language — their specific situation |
| **Option 1 — Brand Sprint** | Scope, deliverables, price, timeline (4–6 weeks, one-time) |
| **Option 2 — Growth Retainer** | Monthly scope, deliverables, price, 3-month minimum |
| **Option 3 — Full Partner** | Full-service monthly scope, price, 6-month engagement |
| **Case Study** | 2 relevant client examples — industry, pain, outcome, metric |
| **Next Step** | One clear CTA — "Book a call to confirm scope" or "Sign here to get started" |

**Pricing philosophy:** Never present a single option. Three tiers give the prospect a choice between yes options, not a choice between yes and no.
