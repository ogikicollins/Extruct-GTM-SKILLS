## THE DISCO CALL — Minute-by-Minute

---

### OPEN (Minutes 1–3): Confirm, earn, orient

> "Hey [Name], thanks for making time. Before we get into anything, quick question — how did you hear about SELLL, and what made you book the call today?"

**Why this matters:** Their answer tells you everything. If they say "LinkedIn," they're digitally engaged. If they say "referral," there's a warm relationship you can use. If they say "Google," they were actively searching — highest intent possible. Whatever they say, it tells you how fast to move.

Then:
> "Perfect. Here's what I'd like to do today — I want to spend the first half understanding where QR Floor Genie is right now in terms of pipeline and how you're currently getting customers. Then I'll show you exactly what a SELLL system for your business would look like. Does that work?"

---

### SECTION 1 — Their Current State (Minutes 3–15)

Work through these in conversation, not as a checklist. One answer leads to the next.

**Q1 — The anchor question:**
> "So walk me through how QR Floor Genie gets a new customer today. What does that path look like from first contact to signed up?"

*What you're listening for:* Referral only? Cold outreach? Inbound from website? Word of mouth at trade shows? This answer defines everything you propose.

**Q2 — Volume:**
> "How many new customers are you bringing on per month right now — and how many do you need to hit your goals?"

*What you're listening for:* If they say "1–2 per month," the gap is clear. If they say "we don't really know," there's no measurement system — that's also a signal.

**Q3 — The demo funnel:**
> "When a flooring store owner does get into a demo with you, what's the close rate like? Do they tend to convert?"

*What you're listening for:* If close rate is high, the problem is purely pipeline. You're not fixing their pitch — you're filling their calendar. This is the most important answer on the call.

**Q4 — Current activity:**
> "Outside of referrals or whoever stumbles onto your website, are you doing any active outreach to flooring stores right now? Cold email, calls, trade shows, anything?"

*What you're listening for:* If the answer is "not really" or "we tried a few things but nothing systematic," that's the confirmation you need.

**Q5 — The competitor question (if they don't raise it):**
> "Are you familiar with Showroom Pricing? Have you run into them in deals?"

*What you're listening for:* Their reaction to Showroom Pricing tells you how they think about competition. If they're worried, urgency exists. If they're dismissive, probe gently — Showroom Pricing was featured on national TV and has a longer runway.

**Q6 — The target question:**
> "When you think about the ideal flooring store that's a perfect fit for QR Floor Genie — what does that look like? Size, type of store, geography?"

*What you're listening for:* This confirms or refines the ICP. Single-location indie? Multi-location chain? Carpet-focused? Luxury tile? This shapes the sequence targeting.

**Q7 — The budget signal:**
> "What's the investment a flooring store typically makes with QR Floor Genie — ballpark monthly?"

*What you're listening for:* This tells you what ARPU looks like, which feeds the ROI math you'll use when you close. Also tells you what THEY can afford to spend on SELLL.

---

### SECTION 2 — Their Goals (Minutes 15–20)

**Q8 — The number:**
> "If you had to pick a number — new flooring store customers per month that would make you feel like the business is really moving — what is that?"

*What you're listening for:* Get a specific number. 5? 10? 20? This becomes the output target for your pipeline projection.

**Q9 — Timeline:**
> "Is there a timeframe that matters here? A milestone, a funding decision, a competitive moment — anything that makes the next 90 days more important than usual?"

*What you're listening for:* Urgency signals. If they're worried about Showroom Pricing eating their market, or if they're trying to hit a revenue target to raise, that sharpens the ask.

**Q10 — The open question:**
> "Is there anything about where QR Floor Genie is right now that I haven't asked about that you think I should know before I show you what we'd build?"

*What you're listening for:* Surprises. Constraints. Budget concerns. Team limitations. Let them talk.

---

### SECTION 3 — THE SELLL PITCH (Minutes 20–35)

Only after you've fully heard them. Do not start this section early.

---

**REFRAME FIRST (30 seconds):**

> "Here's what I'm hearing. You have a product that converts well in demo. You have 11,900 flooring stores in the US who are still on paper pricing — which is literally the problem you solve. And you have no systematic way to get in front of them. That's not a product problem. That's a pipeline problem. And that's exactly what we build."

---

**THE THREE THINGS SELLL BUILDS FOR THEM:**

**1. Signal-triggered sequences targeting flooring store owners**

> "We identify the flooring stores most likely to buy right now — not all 11,900 at once, but the ones showing buying signals: new store openings, stores that just posted a job for a showroom manager, stores that recently got a bad Google review about pricing confusion, stores within 90 days of the major trade events. We build sequences that hit those stores specifically."

**2. A phone-first outreach system**

> "Here's something different about your ICP vs. a typical SaaS company: flooring store owners pick up the phone. They've been in the trade for 20 years. They don't live on LinkedIn. A well-placed cold call from someone who understands their pricing problem will convert better than any email campaign alone. The Growth plan includes cold calling as a core channel — and for your vertical, that matters."

**3. The demo as the close**

> "We're not selling your product. We're selling the demo. Our sequences have one job: get a flooring store owner to agree to watch you show them what a QR code does to their showroom floor. You close from there. Your product is visual. It's immediate. It sells itself. We just need to get the right 20 people per month into that demo."

---

**THE PIPELINE MATH:**

> "Let's run the numbers together. If we put 300 flooring store owners per month into your outreach — signal-triggered, so these are people who are actively growing or updating their showroom — and you get a 4% reply rate... that's 12 conversations a month. If half of those convert to a demo, that's 6 demos. If you close 30% of demos... that's 2 new customers a month. At whatever your monthly ACV is, how does that math look against the investment in SELLL?"

*Let them do the math. Their number is more convincing than yours.*

---

**THE CLOSE (Minutes 35–40):**

> "Based on everything you've told me, here's what I'd recommend. We start with the Growth plan — it includes the cold calling component which I think matters for your vertical, it runs all three channels, and we can be live in 14 days. Month 1 is infrastructure and sequence build. Month 2 the outreach is running. Month 30 you have your first outbound-sourced flooring store customers on the books."

Then:
> "What would need to be true for this to be a yes today?"

Shut up and wait for the answer. Whatever they say, that's the real objection.

## OBJECTIONS — WHAT TO EXPECT

---

**"We don't have the budget for this right now."**

> "Understood. Let me ask you this — if we get you 2 new flooring store customers per month, what does that do to your revenue? [Wait for their number.] So we'd be paying for ourselves in [X] months. The question isn't really budget — it's how fast you want to cover the cost. The Foundation plan gets you into the system at a lower entry point. We start there, prove the numbers, and scale from there."

---

**"We've tried outreach before and it didn't work."**

> "Tell me more about what you tried. [Let them explain.] The difference between what you tried and what SELLL builds is the signal layer. You weren't targeting flooring stores that were showing buying signals — you were targeting all flooring stores. That's the difference between spray-and-pray and signal-triggered. One converts. One doesn't."

---

**"We're not sure the timing is right."**

> "Here's the timing question I'd put back to you: Showroom Pricing has been around since 2020, they just got national TV coverage, and they have a 35-year flooring industry veteran as co-founder. Every month you wait on outbound is a month they compound. You don't need to out-budget them. You need to out-reach them. And you can start that in 14 days."

---

**"I need to think about it."**

> "Of course. What's the one thing you'd need to see or know to feel confident about this? Let's answer that now if we can."

---

**"Can you send me some information first?"**

> "Yes — and I'll make it specific to QR Floor Genie. What I'll send you is a one-page build plan showing exactly what we'd build for your ICP, the 3 sequences, the channel mix, and what the pipeline projections look like at conservative and realistic. I can have that to you today. Fair?"

## WHAT TO LISTEN FOR — THE REAL QUALIFIERS

By the end of the call you need to know:

| Question | What a good answer sounds like |
|---|---|
| How they're getting customers now | "Mostly referrals. Some word of mouth at shows. Nothing systematic." |
| Demo-to-close rate | "When we get someone in a demo, they usually convert." |
| Monthly customer goal | A specific number: "We want to hit 10 new customers per month." |
| Budget | They can name a number, or their ACV x 2 new customers/month > SELLL cost |
| Decision authority | "I make the call." (Founder-selling = single decision maker) |
| Timeline urgency | Any mention of a competitor, a funding target, or a season |

**Red flags:**
- "We have a committee that needs to review this" (unlikely at this stage, but possible if they brought on investors)
- "We want to focus on inbound first" (they don't understand that inbound is broken without a pipeline system feeding it)
- "Our product isn't quite ready for scale yet" (if the demo doesn't convert yet, outbound floods a broken funnel)
