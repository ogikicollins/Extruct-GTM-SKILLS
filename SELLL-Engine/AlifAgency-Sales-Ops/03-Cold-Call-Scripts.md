## Context

Cold calling at ALIF serves two purposes:
1. **Warm calling** — following up with positive email repliers who haven't booked yet
2. **Cold outbound** — calling net-new ICP prospects (secondary to email, used for MENA where WhatsApp isn't available or to break through noise)

All scripts are written in ALIF's tone: direct, confident, peer-to-peer. No corporate language.

---

## Script 1: Warm Call — Positive Email Replier (No Booking Yet)

**Use when:** Prospect replied positively to the Apex/email sequence but hasn't booked. Call within 24 hours of the reply.

**Before the call — prep in 60 seconds:**
- Their name, company, what they do
- What email they replied to (which sequence, which step)
- What they said in the reply
- One thing specific about their business (Instagram, ads, website)

---

**Opening:**

"Hey [First Name], it's Collins from ALIF Agency — you replied to one of our emails recently. Did I catch you at a bad time?"

*[If yes: "No problem — when's better? I'll call you then."]*
*[If no: proceed]*

"Great. So I saw your reply and I didn't want to just ping you another email — figured I'd just call. You mentioned [reference what they said]. Tell me a bit more about that — what's the situation?"

*[Let them talk. Don't interrupt. Listen for the pain.]*

**Qualify in 2 questions:**
1. "What's your current setup for [ads/social/creative] — are you handling it in-house or working with someone?"
2. "And what's the main thing you're trying to fix or improve right now?"

**Bridge to booking:**

"Okay — so what we typically do is a quick 20-minute call where I'll run you through what we've been seeing work for [similar companies]. No pitch — just want to make sure it's relevant before I waste your time. Are you free [Tuesday at 2pm] or [Wednesday morning]?"

*[Give two specific options. Never say "what works for you" — it creates decision fatigue.]*

*[If yes: "Perfect. I'll send the Calendly link right now — you'll get a confirmation. Anything specific you want me to cover?"]*
*[If pushback: see Objection Handlers below]*

## Script 2: Cold Call — Net New ICP Prospect

**Use when:** Calling a company that fits the ICP but hasn't been emailed yet, or calling a decision maker at a company that was emailed but never replied.

**Opening:**

"Hey [First Name], it's Collins from ALIF Agency in Dubai — I'll be honest, this is a cold call. You have 30 seconds to tell me to get lost. [pause / slight laugh]"

*[This disarms them. Most people laugh and stay on.]*

"The reason I'm calling is we've been working with [Devolon / Holz Concepts / Flow Meditation — pick closest match to their industry] on their creative and performance setup and the results were [specific result]. I looked at [their Instagram / their ads] and I think there's a similar opportunity for [company name]."

"I'm not trying to sell you anything on this call. I just want to know if what we're seeing is a problem for you too."

*[Ask one open question:]*
"Who's handling your [paid ads / social content / brand creative] right now — is that in-house or external?"

*[Listen. Qualify. Bridge to booking same as Script 1.]*

## Script 3: Follow-Up Call — No-Show Recovery

**Use when:** Prospect booked a call and didn't show up.

"Hey [First Name], it's Collins from ALIF — we had a call scheduled for [time] today and I think there was a mix-up on the calendar. Completely fine. Just wanted to check if you'd still like to connect this week?"

*[Keep it short. No guilt. Two options:]*

"I have [Wednesday at 3pm] or [Thursday morning] — does either work?"

## Voicemail SOPs

### Voicemail 1: The Competitor Trick

**When to use:** Cold calling a prospect who hasn't responded to emails. Use on Call Attempt 2 or 3.

**Script:**

"Hey [First Name], Collins here from ALIF Agency — I was calling regarding your competitor [name a direct competitor if you know one, or say 'one of the other [industry] brands in [city/region]']. Give me a call back when you get a moment — [your number]. That's [repeat number slowly]. Thanks."

**Why it works:** Curiosity is the strongest callback trigger. "Regarding your competitor" creates an immediate open loop — they need to know what you know. Don't explain on the voicemail. Let the mystery do the work.

**Important:** When they call back, you say: "Thanks for calling back. So I was looking at [their brand] alongside a few others in the [industry] space and I noticed [specific observation about their ads/content/website]. I thought it was worth a quick conversation. Is now a good time?"

---

### Voicemail 2: The Audit Hook

**When to use:** For warm prospects (positive email repliers who haven't booked). After 2 unanswered call attempts.

**Script:**

"Hey [First Name], Collins from ALIF — I actually pulled a quick audit on [company name]'s [Instagram / ad account / website] and found a couple of things worth sharing. Nothing complicated, just [one specific finding — e.g., 'your last 3 ads have been running 45+ days without a creative refresh, which is typically where performance starts dropping']. Happy to walk you through it — [number]. That's [repeat number]. Speak soon."

**Why it works:** You've done work for them before they've given you anything. It's a tangible gift, not a pitch. The specific finding proves you're not generic.

---

### Voicemail 3: The Peer-to-Peer Direct

**When to use:** For decision maker prospects (CMO/Founder level). When you want to cut through.

**Script:**

"[First Name], Collins from ALIF in Dubai. Quick call — not trying to sell you anything. Just think there's something relevant to [company name] based on what we've been seeing in [their industry] lately. Worth 15 minutes. [Number]. Thanks."

**Keep it under 20 seconds. No enthusiasm. Confident and matter-of-fact.**

## Objection Handlers (Phone)

**"We're already working with an agency"**
"Completely understand — most of the brands we work with had someone before us. I'm not asking you to switch anything. I just want to understand if what we're doing is relevant to a problem you actually have. One question: are you happy with the results you're getting right now?"

**"Send me an email"**
"I will — but just so I can make it worth reading, what's the one thing you're most focused on fixing in the next 90 days? [Their answer] — perfect. I'll send something specific to that. What's the best email?"

**"Not interested"**
"Totally fair — can I ask what's making it a no? Not trying to change your mind, just want to understand so I don't waste your time in the future." [Listen. This is intelligence.]

**"How did you get my number?"**
"[LinkedIn / Apollo / direct research]. We're pretty targeted about who we reach out to — you fit a very specific profile we look for. Is that a problem?"

**"I'm busy"**
"Completely fine — I'll be 60 seconds. If it's not relevant I'll let you go. [One sentence on why you called]. Does that sound like something worth 20 minutes?"
