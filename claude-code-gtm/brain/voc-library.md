## How to Use This Library

**Before writing any email:** Check the relevant pain category. Use their language, not yours.
**Before writing a subject line:** Scan "exact phrases" in each category — buyers recognize their own words.
**Before a discovery call:** Read the relevant section to calibrate what you already know they'll say.

## Pain Category 1: Broken Outbound / Low Reply Rates

**Persona match:** CRO, VP Sales, Scaling Founder
**Hypothesis match:** H4 (Broken Sequence), H7 (Stuck Audit)

| Quote | Source | Persona | Date | How to Use |
|-------|--------|---------|------|-----------|
| "The reply rates are embarrassing." | Michael Chen, VP Sales, DataFlow Analytics | VP Sales | 2026-06-21 | Email subject / body opener. The word "embarrassing" = shame signal, not just metric. |
| *(add from future calls)* | | | | |

**Pattern in this category:** Buyers say "embarrassing" or "underwhelming" — never just "bad." They're not describing a metric, they're describing shame. Don't respond with a metric. Respond with an explanation.

**Hook formulas from this pain:**
- "The reply rates aren't the problem — they're the symptom."
- "Most [VP Sales / CROs] I talk to describe their reply rates as embarrassing. Here's why it's not a messaging problem."
- "What's your outbound reply rate right now? [pause] Most I talk to say under 1%."

## Pain Category 2: Founder Still in Every Deal

**Persona match:** Scaling Founder, Bootstrapped CEO
**Hypothesis match:** H3 (Founder Ceiling)

| Quote | Source | Persona | Date | How to Use |
|-------|--------|---------|------|-----------|
| *(add from calls)* | | | | |

**Pattern:** Founders in this situation deflect. They say "I'm still hands-on with sales" as if it's a choice, not a constraint. They rarely say "I can't get out of the deals." Meet them where they are — acknowledge the hands-on involvement before offering the exit.

**Hook formulas:**
- "At [X] ARR, most founders are still in 80% of deals. That's not a leadership problem — it's a systems problem."
- "The moment you step out of a deal and it closes anyway — that's when the business becomes scalable."

## Pain Category 3: SDR Productivity / Research Time Sink

**Persona match:** CRO, VP Sales
**Hypothesis match:** H4, H2 (SDR Hiring Trap)

| Quote | Source | Persona | Date | How to Use |
|-------|--------|---------|------|-----------|
| *(add from calls)* | | | | |

**Pattern:** When asked about SDR time allocation, buyers guess around 30%. The actual number (when tracked) is usually 45–55%. Lead with the higher estimate — they always confirm it's at least that bad.

**Hook formulas:**
- "Your SDRs are probably spending 40–50% of their time not selling. Here's the math on what that costs."
- "The best SDRs I've seen spend 20% on research. Most B2B teams run at 45–50%."

## Pain Category 4: Post-Raise Pipeline Pressure

**Persona match:** CRO, Scaling Founder
**Hypothesis match:** H1 (Post-Raise Pressure)

| Quote | Source | Persona | Date | How to Use |
|-------|--------|---------|------|-----------|
| *(add from calls)* | | | | |

**Pattern:** Buyers in this category rarely name the board pressure directly. They say "we need to show growth" or "this quarter matters." They're proxy statements for board accountability. Don't push on the board — push on what the growth looks like.

**Hook formulas:**
- "The quarter after a funding round is usually the most important one."
- "Investors don't track ARR monthly — they track pipeline quarterly. What's your pipeline look like right now?"

## Pain Category 5: New Role / Inheriting a Broken Stack

**Persona match:** Newly Hired VP Sales
**Hypothesis match:** H5 (New VP Sales Window), H6 (Lean Rebuild)

| Quote | Source | Persona | Date | How to Use |
|-------|--------|---------|------|-----------|
| "Lots of opportunity but also lots to untangle." | Michael Chen (LinkedIn post), DataFlow | VP Sales | 2026-06-21 | LinkedIn comment response / Email 2 opener |
| *(add from calls)* | | | | |

**Pattern:** "Lots to untangle" is the VP Sales equivalent of "it's complicated." They're describing inherited complexity without blaming the previous person. Don't push on blame — push on what the clean version looks like.

**Hook formulas:**
- "The first 30 days in a new VP Sales seat usually reveals the same 3 things. What have you found?"
- "The observations you're making right now are the clearest you'll ever see the system — it gets harder after 90 days when you're inside it."
- "'Lots to untangle' — what does it look like when it's untangled?"

## Phrases That Kill Deals

Words and phrases that appeared in calls where the conversation stalled or the prospect went cold.

| Phrase | Why It Kills | What to Say Instead |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| "We're a full-service GTM agency" | Generic — every agency says it | "We build one thing: a signal-driven outbound engine in 90 days." |
| "AI-powered outreach" | Every vendor says this | "Intent-triggered outreach — emails that fire because something happened at their company today" |
| "We help B2B companies grow" | Meaningless at any stage | "We build the system that converts your ICP's signals into booked meetings" |
| "End-to-end solution" | Sounds like buzzword | Just describe the 90-day build specifically |
| "Our proprietary methodology" | Sounds like a black box | "Here's exactly what we build and in what order" |
| "Guaranteed results" | Doesn't land without specifics | "Here's what Devolon saw in 90 days — [specific stat]" |
| "Checking in" | No value, sounds weak | Lead with a new fact or a question |
| "Just following up" | Same | Same |
