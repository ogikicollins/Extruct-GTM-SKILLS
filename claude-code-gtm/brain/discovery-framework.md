# Discovery Call Framework — SELLL.io
> Brain Layer: Conversational | Updated: 2026-06-21
> Used by: meeting-automation (pre-call brief), call-intelligence (post-call analysis), deal-nurture (Stage 2 entry gate)

The discovery call has one job: determine whether this prospect has (1) a real problem, (2) budget authority, (3) a timeline, and (4) a willingness to act. Every question in this framework is designed to reveal one of those four dimensions.

**Time budget:** 25–30 minutes total. 5 minutes rapport, 15 minutes discovery, 5 minutes demo/proof, 5 minutes next steps.

---

## Pre-Call Setup (Auto-generated by meeting-automation)

Before every discovery call, the brain generates a pre-call brief with:
- 60-second company summary (what they do, stage, size)
- Buyer profile (who they are, how long in role, LinkedIn recent activity)
- Why now (which signal triggered them entering the pipeline)
- Tech stack (what tools they're using based on enrichment)
- Hypothesis match (which of the 7 hypotheses applies)
- Likely objections (top 2-3 from the objection bank for their persona)
- Suggested opening observation (personalized to their situation)
- Relevant proof point (best client outcome to reference for their vertical + pain)
- Call agenda (5-part structure below)

## Call Agenda (5 Parts)

**Share this at the top of the call:**
> "I have 25 minutes blocked — here's what I'd like to cover: first, I want to understand what's actually happening with your pipeline right now. Then I'll share what we're seeing that's relevant, show you a quick proof point, and at the end we'll figure out together if it makes sense to go deeper. Sound good?"

## Discovery Questions (20 Questions Across 4 Dimensions)

### Dimension 1: The Problem (Questions 1–6)

**Q1: The Situation Question**
> "Walk me through what your outbound motion looks like right now — what's working, what's not."

*What you're listening for:* Manual list-building, low reply rates, inconsistent pipeline, founder still selling, SDRs doing research instead of selling.
*Green flag:* They immediately name a specific pain ("our reply rates are 0.5%")
*Red flag:* "Everything is actually pretty good" — probe harder or disqualify

---

**Q2: The Volume Question**
> "How many personalized outbound conversations is your team having per day right now?"

*What you're listening for:* Anything under 50/day signals a process problem.
*Green flag:* "Maybe 30-40" or "honestly I don't know" — both indicate gap
*Red flag:* "We're doing 150+ already" — system may be working, qualify further

---

**Q3: The Time Allocation Question**
> "Roughly what percentage of your SDRs' time is spent on actual selling versus research, list-building, and admin?"

*What you're listening for:* Anything over 30% in research is a strong signal.
*Green flag:* "Probably 40-50%" — Devolon before-state, perfect
*Red flag:* "We have RevOps handling all the research" — they may already have a solution

---

**Q4: The Reply Rate Question**
> "What are you seeing for reply rates on your cold outreach right now?"

*What you're listening for:* Industry average is 0.5-1%. Anything below 2% is a strong signal.
*Green flag:* "Maybe 1% or less" — directly validates H4 (broken sequence)
*Red flag:* "We're at 4-5%" — they may have a better system than we thought; probe for consistency

---

**Q5: The Pain History Question**
> "Have you tried to solve this before — outside tools, agencies, building in-house? What happened?"

*What you're listening for:* Prior attempts + failure = pre-sold on the problem. No prior attempts = still in early awareness.
*Green flag:* "We tried Belkins for 6 months" or "We used a consultant but got a document, not a system"
*Red flag:* "No we've never really tried anything" — may not have urgency to act

---

**Q6: The Impact Question**
> "What does the pipeline gap cost you right now — in missed targets, stalled growth, or headcount you're holding off on hiring?"

*What you're listening for:* Dollar amount or tangible business consequence. Vague answers = low urgency.
*Green flag:* "We missed Q2 by 30%" or "I'm holding off on hiring another AE because I don't have enough pipeline to feed them"
*Red flag:* "Honestly it's fine, we're just looking to improve" — weak pain, proceed cautiously

---

### Dimension 2: The Goal (Questions 7–10)

**Q7: The Success Definition Question**
> "If we built the system and it worked exactly as you hoped — what would be different 90 days from now?"

*What you're listening for:* Specific, measurable target. Vague answers = unclear vision = harder to close.
*Green flag:* "I want 20 qualified meetings per month by Q4" — specific, actionable
*Red flag:* "Just more pipeline" — need to make this specific before the call ends

---

**Q8: The Priority Question**
> "On a scale of 1-10, how important is solving this in the next 90 days versus everything else on your plate?"

*What you're listening for:* 7+ = active priority. Below 6 = they're comparing but not committed.
*Green flag:* 8-9 with a specific reason ("board review in October")
*Red flag:* "Maybe a 6" — de-prioritize this account in the pipeline; watch for a better trigger

---

**Q9: The Alternative Question**
> "What happens if you don't solve this in the next 90 days? Is there a plan B?"

*What you're listening for:* No plan B = higher urgency. Strong plan B = lower urgency.
*Green flag:* "We'll have to hire more SDRs at $80K each, and we can't afford that"
*Red flag:* "We could probably just increase our Apollo budget" — weak urgency, they think there's an easy alternative

---

**Q10: The Blocking Question**
> "Is there anything that would prevent you from moving forward in the next 2 weeks if this looks right?"

*What you're listening for:* Real blockers (budget cycle, board approval) vs. stall tactics.
*Green flag:* Specific blocker with a timeline ("budget renews in August")
*Red flag:* "I'd just need to think about it" — no specific blocker = soft no; find the real concern

---

### Dimension 3: The Budget (Questions 11–14)

**Q11: The Current Spend Question**
> "What are you currently spending on your sales stack — tools, SDR salaries, and any external services?"

*What you're listening for:* Total GTM budget signals ability to spend. $200K+ in sales infrastructure = definitely has budget.
*Green flag:* "We spend about $500K on our sales team and tools"
*Red flag:* "We're pretty lean — just using the free version of HubSpot" — budget may be too tight

---

**Q12: The Budget Authority Question**
> "Is this a decision you can make, or does it go to someone else for approval?"

*What you're listening for:* Identify the economic buyer early.
*Green flag:* "Yes this is my budget to allocate" — proceed to close
*Yellow flag:* "I'd need CEO sign-off" — ask to include CEO in next meeting
*Red flag:* "I'd have to go to a committee" — timeline will be long; adjust accordingly

---

**Q13: The Investment Framing Question**
> "When you're evaluating something like this, are you thinking about it as a monthly expense or a return on investment?"

*What you're listening for:* ROI-minded = use the calculator. Cost-minded = start with cost of not doing it.
*Green flag:* "ROI — I want to see the math"
*Red flag:* "We have a strict budget cap" — anchor to the cost of misallocated SDR time first

---

**Q14: The Timeline Question**
> "When would you want this up and running, ideally?"

*What you're listening for:* Specific date = real urgency. "Someday" = nurture.
*Green flag:* "Before end of Q3" or "before we hire the next SDR"
*Red flag:* "No rush, whenever" — low urgency, extend the sequence timeline

---

### Dimension 4: The Fit (Questions 15–18)

**Q15: The ICP Confirmation Question**
> "Quick confirm — you're [describe their company in one sentence]. Is that still the right description of where you are?"

*What you're listening for:* Confirms or corrects your ICP scoring.
*Why it matters:* Sometimes enrichment data is wrong. Asking also signals you've done research.

---

**Q16: The Tech Stack Confirmation Question**
> "You're running [CRM] and [sequencer] — is that still accurate? Any recent changes to your stack?"

*What you're listening for:* Confirms technographic fit. New stack changes can change integration complexity.

---

**Q17: The Team Question**
> "Who on your team would be most involved in using the system once it's built — is it primarily you, or is there a RevOps or sales ops person?"

*What you're listening for:* Identifies the Thread B champion. If there's a RevOps person, add them to the multi-thread and the implementation conversation.

---

**Q18: The Competitive Question**
> "Are you looking at anyone else for this, or is this an exploratory conversation?"

*What you're listening for:* Active evaluation = decision timeline is shorter. Exploratory = longer.
*Green flag:* "We're comparing you to Belkins" — use competitive battle card
*Red flag:* "We're looking at about 6 agencies" — may not be serious; qualify the decision timeline

---

### Bonus Dimension: The Close Setup (Questions 19–20)

**Q19: The Decision Process Question**
> "If this conversation goes well, what does the path to a decision look like from your side?"

*What you're listening for:* Number of steps, number of stakeholders, timeline.
*Ideal answer:* "I'd need to see a proposal and then it's my call" — one step, fast
*Complex answer:* "I'd need to get my CEO, CFO, and head of Marketing aligned" — multi-stakeholder; map the buying committee

---

**Q20: The First Commitment Test**
> "Based on what you've heard, does this feel like it could be the right direction? What questions do you need answered before you'd feel comfortable moving forward?"

*What you're listening for:* Unvoiced objections surface here. The question they ask reveals the blocker.
*Ideal answer:* "I'd like to see the case study in more detail and then I'm ready to talk scope"
*Red flag:* "I need to think about it" — probe: "What specifically are you thinking through?"

## Post-Call Protocol (5 Steps in 2 Hours)

**Step 1 (0–15 min after call):** Log the qualification score and key notes in `state.md`

**Step 2 (0–30 min after call):** Identify which objection from the objection bank was raised. Log in `institutional-memory/`

**Step 3 (within 2 hours):** Send the post-call email (template in deal-nurture Stage 1). Reference something specific said on the call.

**Step 4 (same day):** If qualified (4.0+), draft the proposal. If conditional (3.0–3.9), schedule the follow-up email addressing the specific blocker.

**Step 5 (within 24h):** Update `brain/institutional-memory/campaigns.md` with the conversation pattern. Did the discovery questions land? What surprised you? What new objection appeared that isn't in the bank?
