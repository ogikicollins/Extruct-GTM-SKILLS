# Fintech / Financial SaaS — Vertical Playbook
> Brain Layer: Intelligence | Updated: 2026-06-21
> Applies to: Banking software, payments platforms, lending tech, insurtech, compliance tech, financial data platforms

---

## Vertical ICP Nuance

Fintech SaaS at $2M–$30M ARR typically has:
- Longer sales cycles (12–16 weeks) due to compliance review requirements
- Smaller buying committees but with a legal/compliance stakeholder who can veto anything
- Higher average ACV ($25K–$60K) — more cautious buyers, but bigger deals
- Strong preference for vendors that look enterprise-grade, even if the vendor is small
- "Try before you buy" culture is lower here — they want proof before risk

**Scoring adjustment for Fintech:**
- Add 10 points if they have a dedicated compliance team (usually > 50 employees or regulated product)
- Subtract 10 points if the product touches consumer financial data — CCPA/GDPR conversations will slow every deal
- Add 5 points if they're at Series A or later — funding rounds signal board-level pipeline pressure

---

## Specific Pain Angle

**What resonates most:** Pipeline predictability in a high-trust, compliance-conscious sale.

Fintech buyers aren't just worried about reply rates — they're worried about reaching the right person at a regulated institution without triggering a compliance concern on the prospect's side. Their SDRs often use the same generic sequences built for SaaS companies, which don't account for how differently financial buyers respond.

**Best opening angle:**
> "Most Fintech outbound motions weren't built for a compliance-conscious buyer. The timing windows are different, the language is different, and what lands at a bank is not what lands at a growth-stage SaaS company. We build the motion to account for that."

## Best Proof Point to Lead With

**Current best match:** Devolon (reply rate improvement, SDR productivity)
→ Frame it as: "If Devolon's generic SaaS motion was getting this wrong — what does that mean for a regulated vertical where the stakes are higher?"

**Ideal future proof point (prioritize when you close a Fintech client):** Document the compliance-specific approach, the stakeholder map, and the exact outreach angle that worked. This is the most valuable case study for the vertical.

**If asked for vertical-specific proof:** Lead with process transparency — "Here's exactly what we build, step by step. You can see every component before committing to anything."

## Specific Objections in Fintech

| Objection | Frequency | Counter | Proof Point |
|-----------|-----------|---------|------------|
| "We have strict policies on data security — how do you handle our prospect data?" | High | "We don't store your prospect data. Lists are built fresh for each campaign, used in the sequence, and stored in your CRM under your control. We access what we need to build the system — you own everything after." | Process walkthrough |
| "Our compliance team needs to review any new vendor" | Medium | "Completely standard — we work with regulated companies. Happy to complete a vendor questionnaire and join a call with your compliance team whenever they're ready." | Offer questionnaire proactively |
| "We already have a compliance-trained SDR team that knows our product" | Medium | "Perfect — we're not replacing them. We're building the intelligence layer that tells them who to call and when. Their domain knowledge goes to the right conversations instead of being wasted on cold research." | Devolon SDR efficiency |
| "We can't use external tools that touch our prospect database" | Medium | "We work with your existing infrastructure. We build on top of what you have — no new data stores, no third-party data sharing that isn't already in your stack." | Tech stack walkthrough |

## Fintech Vertical Notes

- Reference Fintech-specific compliance standards when relevant: SOC 2, ISO 27001, PCI DSS. Showing fluency with these signals that SELLL understands the world they operate in.
- Never ask a Fintech buyer to share prospect data or CRM data before a contract is signed.
- Fintech boards care deeply about CAC and LTV — frame ROI math in those terms, not just "meetings booked."
