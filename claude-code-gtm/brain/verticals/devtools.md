# DevTools / Developer Platforms — Vertical Playbook
> Brain Layer: Intelligence | Updated: 2026-06-21
> Applies to: Developer tooling, API platforms, infrastructure SaaS, security tools, data engineering, CI/CD platforms, observability tools

---

## Vertical ICP Nuance

DevTools companies have a unique GTM problem:
- PLG (product-led growth) is often the primary motion — outbound is secondary or non-existent
- The end user (developer/engineer) is different from the economic buyer (CTO, VP Engineering, CFO)
- Developers are allergic to sales — but engineering leaders are standard enterprise buyers
- Buying decisions require proof-of-concept or free-tier adoption before progressing to enterprise
- Short PLG sales cycles, but 10–16 week enterprise cycles

**The core pattern:** PLG brings in usage, but converting that usage to enterprise contracts requires outbound to reach the economic buyer — who is not the developer using the free tier. This is the "PLG-to-enterprise gap" and almost every DevTools company in the $2M–$15M ARR range is stuck in it.

**Scoring adjustments for DevTools:**
- Add 15 points if they have an active free tier / PLG motion with no enterprise sales motion yet
- Add 10 points if they have a VP Sales or CRO hire (signal: they've acknowledged the gap)
- Subtract 10 points if they're pure PLG with no enterprise aspiration — not our timing yet
- Add 5 points if the company is post-Series A (board is asking about enterprise revenue)

## Specific Pain Angle

**What resonates most:** The PLG-to-enterprise conversion gap.

DevTools companies grow bottom-up — a developer discovers the tool, loves it, starts using it. But converting that individual usage to a team or enterprise contract requires reaching the person with budget authority, which is NOT the developer. Engineering leaders and CFOs make the enterprise buying decision. They are reachable by outbound — but most DevTools companies don't know how to talk to them in a way that resonates.

**Best opening angle:**
> "Developer tools grow by PLG. Enterprise contracts come from a different motion — one that reaches the VP Engineering or CFO who controls the budget, and frames the conversation in business outcomes rather than technical features. We build that bridge."

## DevTools Vertical Notes

- Never use the word "sales" with a developer-adjacent buyer. Use "commercial motion," "enterprise motion," or "growth system."
- "Converting usage to revenue" is the most resonant framing in this vertical.
- The discovery call question that reveals the most: "What % of your free-tier users eventually convert to a paid plan — and how does that happen?"
- Frame SELLL's system as serving the enterprise buyer, not disrupting the PLG motion.
- If they have a DevRel team but no enterprise motion — that's the exact gap. The DevRel team created awareness. Now they need conversion infrastructure.
