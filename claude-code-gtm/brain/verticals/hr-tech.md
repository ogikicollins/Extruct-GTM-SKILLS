# HR Tech — Vertical Playbook
> Brain Layer: Intelligence | Updated: 2026-06-21
> Applies to: HRIS platforms, recruiting software, L&D tools, performance management SaaS, workforce analytics, compensation platforms

---

## Vertical ICP Nuance

HR Tech at $2M–$30M ARR typically has:
- Longer evaluation cycles — People Ops and HR leaders are consensus-driven and risk-averse
- 2–3 stakeholder groups: CHRO/CPO (champion), Finance (budget), IT (integration approval)
- High competition — HR Tech buyers have been over-pitched for years and have strong pattern recognition for templated outreach
- Strongest ROI language: time-to-hire, retention rate, HR cost per employee — not pipeline or ARR
- Buying decision often sits with the CEO at companies without a full HR executive

**Scoring adjustments for HR Tech:**
- Subtract 5 points if there's no dedicated CHRO or CPO — find the actual decision maker before outreach
- Add 10 points if the company just crossed 50 employees (triggered HR infrastructure needs)
- Add 10 points if they recently posted VP People or Director of HR jobs (scaling HR = scaling infrastructure need)

---

## Specific Pain Angle

**What resonates most:** Cutting through the noise in the most over-pitched vertical.

HR Tech buyers receive more templated outbound than any other buyer type. They've been approached by 15–20 platforms selling similar things. The outreach that works arrives at the exact moment they're solving for a specific problem — not "we have an HR platform" but "you just posted a Director of Talent Acquisition job, which means you're scaling the hiring function, which means [specific pain they'll have in 60 days]."

**Best opening angle:**
> "HR Tech has the most saturated outbound environment of any vertical. Every sequence sounds the same. We build signal-triggered outreach — so the email arrives the week they post a 'Director of People' role (scaling moment) or the month after a headcount announcement. Signal timing changes how they receive it."

## Specific Objections in HR Tech

| Objection | Frequency | Counter | Proof Point |
|-----------|-----------|---------|------------|
| "Our buyers are HR leaders — they hate cold outreach" | High | "True — which is why signal timing matters so much in this vertical. We reach them the week they're actually trying to solve the problem, not on a generic calendar sequence. That changes how they receive it." | Timing framing |
| "We rely mostly on PLG and inbound" | Medium | "PLG serves the companies already looking. Outbound reaches the ones who have the problem but haven't started evaluating yet. At $2M–$10M ARR, that's usually a larger segment than inbound alone." | Pipeline math |
| "We've tried agencies before — they didn't understand our buyer" | High | "That's the most common feedback we get from HR Tech companies. The ICP research phase takes 3 weeks before we send a single email. The buyer profile is built from the ground up — not reused from a previous client." | Process walkthrough |
| "We're focused on enterprise, not SMB" | Medium | "The outbound motion we build is filterable by company size. If enterprise is the target, the ICP gets built around enterprise signals — headcount thresholds, funding stage, tech stack indicators." | ICP customization |

**Groups to monitor:**
- CHRO Network
- HR Leaders Network
- People Operations Community
- HR Technology Conference community

**Hiring signals specific to HR Tech:**

| Job Post | Signal | Action |
|----------|--------|--------|
| "VP People" or "Chief People Officer" | Company scaling HR function | H5 + H2 trigger — they'll need HR tools in 60 days |
| "Director of Talent Acquisition" | High hiring volume, recruiting tools need | H2 trigger |
| "HR Business Partner" × 2+ | HR team scaling rapidly | H1 or H2 depending on stage |
| "Compensation & Benefits Manager" | Maturing compensation program | Compensation platform angle |
| "L&D Manager" or "Training Lead" | L&D investment | L&D platform angle |

**Content that works in HR Tech:** Data-driven takes on hiring trends, retention benchmarks, workforce productivity stats. Avoid motivational HR content — it reads as soft to buyers.

## HR Tech Vertical Notes

- Frame ROI in HR-native language first: "time-to-hire reduction," "HR cost per employee," "candidate quality score" — then connect to revenue impact second.
- CHRO buyers care about team efficiency and candidate experience. CRO/CEO buyers care about revenue enablement and growth. Know which one you're talking to before the call.
- HR Tech companies at Series A often have a CEO who doubles as the sales lead — use Scaling Founder persona framing.
