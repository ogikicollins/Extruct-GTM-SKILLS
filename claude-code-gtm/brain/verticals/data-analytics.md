# Data & Analytics — Vertical Playbook
> Brain Layer: Intelligence | Updated: 2026-06-21
> Applies to: BI platforms, data warehousing, analytics SaaS, ETL/ELT tools, data observability, reporting platforms, data catalog tools

---

## Vertical ICP Nuance

Data & Analytics companies at $2M–$30M ARR:
- Sell primarily to data teams (Data Engineers, Analytics Engineers, Head of Data, CDO)
- Economic buyer is usually the CFO or CTO — not the data team itself
- Strong PLG element in many — free tier → self-serve → enterprise
- Highly competitive landscape: most companies are evaluating 2–3 competing tools simultaneously
- Data buyers are analytical and want to see numbers before they want to hear stories — but they want both

**The typical GTM problem in this vertical:** Strong inbound and PLG, but weak outbound motion to reach enterprise economic buyers. The people using the tool love it. The people controlling the enterprise budget don't know it exists. SDRs often pitch technical features to non-technical buyers — wrong message, wrong person.

**Scoring adjustments for Data & Analytics:**
- Add 10 points if they have a "Head of Data" or CDO hire (scaling the data function = scaling tool investment)
- Add 10 points if they're Series A with usage metrics but no enterprise contract motion yet
- Add 5 points if they're in a highly regulated vertical themselves (Fintech, Healthcare) — data governance is a differentiator
- Subtract 5 points if pure data infrastructure (engineers-only buyers) — hard to build outbound without a business buyer

## Specific Pain Angle

**What resonates most:** Reaching the economic buyer, not just the data team.

Data tools get adopted bottom-up: a data engineer uses the free tier, loves the product, pitches it internally. But converting that adoption to a multi-year enterprise contract requires reaching the CFO or CTO with a business outcome story — not a feature walkthrough. Most Data & Analytics companies don't have the outbound motion to make that connection.

**Best opening angle:**
> "Data tools grow through bottoms-up adoption. But enterprise contracts come from a different conversation — one with the CFO or CTO who frames the decision in terms of data team productivity and business impact, not technical specs. We build the motion that reaches that buyer."

## Best Proof Point to Lead With

**Primary:** Devolon (systematic outbound, 200+ daily conversations) — frame it as "building a systematic motion alongside the product-led motion."

**The unique angle for Data & Analytics:** "The people who love your product can't approve the enterprise contract. We build the bridge between user adoption and enterprise revenue."

**Ideal future proof point:** A Data & Analytics company specifically. When this closes, document: the economic buyer persona, the business outcome framing that worked, and the specific signals that triggered timing.

## Data & Analytics Vertical Notes

- **Lead with numbers first in this vertical** — data buyers are quantitatively minded. The ROI math goes first, the case study goes second.
- The best discovery question: "What % of your enterprise pipeline comes from product-qualified leads vs. outbound?" If they pause — there's no outbound motion yet.
- Mention data quality and accuracy in your tooling description — data buyers are skeptical of any system that touches their data. Reference Prospeo + FullEnrich waterfall and validation steps.
- Frame the system as "the go-to-market intelligence layer" — language that resonates with technical-to-business crossover buyers like CDOs.
- CDOs and Heads of Data think in terms of ROI per analyst — help them see the business case in their own framework.
