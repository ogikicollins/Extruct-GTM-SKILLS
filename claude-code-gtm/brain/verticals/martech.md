# MarTech / SalesTech — Vertical Playbook
> Brain Layer: Intelligence | Updated: 2026-06-21
> Applies to: Marketing automation, sales enablement, intent data, ABM platforms, conversational intelligence, CRM tools, outbound tooling, revenue intelligence

---

## Vertical ICP Nuance

MarTech/SalesTech companies are the hardest outbound target in the ICP. They:
- Know the outbound playbook better than almost any other buyer — they've built or sold similar things
- Will catch generic framing immediately and disengage
- Are highly ROI-literate: they speak in CPL, CAC, pipeline velocity, payback period
- Often have in-house RevOps or strong internal GTM capability
- Are surrounded by people selling GTM services — they're exhausted by vendor pitches

**The paradox:** MarTech companies that can't build an effective outbound motion for themselves are the best possible clients. They know what good looks like. They know they're not there. They're the most motivated to fix it.

**Scoring adjustments for MarTech/SalesTech:**
- Add 15 points if they have SDRs but no signal intelligence layer (sequences running blind)
- Add 10 points if their own product is outbound-adjacent (they understand the value of the stack)
- Subtract 10 points if they have a full RevOps function AND a clear system already — they likely don't need us yet

---

## Specific Pain Angle

**What resonates most:** The gap between having the tools and having the system.

MarTech buyers often have 6–10 tools in their stack and a RevOps person managing them. What they don't have is the layer that connects those tools to actual buyer signals. Their stack is reactive — it executes what they tell it, but doesn't tell them who to reach or when. The SELLL brain is the missing intelligence layer.

**Best opening angle:**
> "You're probably the most tool-literate CRO I'll talk to this month. Which means you know that having Apollo + Outreach + HubSpot doesn't automatically generate pipeline — that requires the signal layer and intelligence model connecting them. We build that layer."

## Best Proof Point to Lead With

**Primary:** Devolon (35 → 200+ daily conversations — the shift from tools to system)
→ Frame it as: "They had the tools. What they didn't have was the intelligence layer that told those tools what to do. That's the only thing we changed."

**Secondary:** Holz Concepts (new CRO, 90-day mandate — resonates for CROs being held to pipeline targets)

## MarTech Vertical Notes

- Be more specific in this vertical than any other. Generic framing gets caught faster here.
- Ask about their stack in the first email — it signals that you understand the tool landscape, and their answer tells you exactly what's missing.
- The best discovery question for MarTech: "What signals are you using to trigger outreach today?" If they pause — that's the gap.
- Never position as "another GTM vendor." Position as the layer that connects their existing investment.
