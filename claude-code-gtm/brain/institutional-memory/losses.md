> Brain Layer: Institutional Knowledge | Updated: 2026-06-21

Every lost deal has a lesson. Most agencies ignore their losses. The brain doesn't.

The most important question after every lost deal is not "why did we lose?" but "what could we have known earlier that would have told us this deal wouldn't close — so we could redirect effort to deals that will?"

**What "fixable" means:**
- Fixable = this loss was caused by a gap in our process (wrong counter, slow follow-up, wrong proof point). We can fix it.
- Not fixable = this was a qualification error. The account was never going to close. Fix the ICP scoring, not the sales process.

## What to Extract From Every Loss

When a deal is marked Closed Lost in `state.md`, run this analysis.

### The 10 Loss Dimensions

**1. Where Did It Die?**
Which stage: Pre-reply (never replied) | Reply (replied but didn't book) | Meeting (met but didn't progress) | Proposal (sent but didn't sign) | Stalled (went dark)

**2. Stated Reason**
What the prospect said when they declined (if they said anything at all).

**3. Real Reason**
What the call notes, email trail, and signals actually suggest. Often different from the stated reason.

**4. Was This Fixable?**
ICP error (wrong account) vs. execution error (right account, wrong approach).

**5. Which Objection Wasn't Resolved?**
What objection killed the deal? Was it in the objection bank? Did we deploy the counter?

**6. Competitor Win**
If they went to a competitor, which one and what did that competitor offer that we didn't?

**7. Timing**
Was this a true "not now" that should be re-engaged in 90 days? Or a permanent no?

**8. ICP Score vs. Outcome**
What was the lead score? Did the model predict this correctly? If a high-scored account didn't close, why?

**9. What We'd Do Differently**
If we could replay the deal from the first email, what would change?

**10. Re-engagement Potential**
Should this go to the re-engagement queue? If yes, what trigger should fire before we reach out again?

## Cross-Loss Pattern Analysis

Run this after every 5 losses. Feed findings back into the ICP, discovery framework, and objection bank.

```
LOSS PATTERN ANALYSIS — [Date] — [N total losses analyzed]

Q1: What percentage of losses were ICP errors (wrong account) vs. execution errors?
→ 
  (If >40% are ICP errors: the lead scoring model is too permissive — tighten criteria)
  (If >60% are execution errors: the sales process needs work, not the list)

Q2: Which stage do we lose most deals?
→ 
  (Pre-reply = targeting or deliverability problem)
  (Reply → Meeting = booking speed or CTA problem)
  (Meeting → Proposal = discovery or qualification problem)
  (Proposal → Close = ROI clarity or competitive positioning problem)

Q3: Which objection appears most in lost deals — and what counter consistently failed?
→ 
  (Rewrite that counter immediately)

Q4: Which competitor are we losing to most often — and what do they say about themselves?
→ 
  (Update that competitor's battle card)

Q5: Are there ICP dimensions that were high but the deal still didn't close?
→ 
  (Reduce the weight of those dimensions in the scoring rubric)

Q6: Which hypotheses are generating accounts that don't convert?
→ 
  (Reduce confidence score for those hypotheses in hypothesis_set.md)

Q7: What is the most common "real reason" for losses (not the stated reason)?
→ 
  (This is the biggest product-market fit signal — if the real reason is always the same,
   it may be a positioning problem, not a sales problem)

Q8: Of all the losses, which are Type C (timing errors) that should be re-engaged?
→ 
  (These are not lost deals — they're deferred pipeline. Work the re-engagement queue.)
```
