## Profile Optimization (One-Time Setup)

### Profile Photo
**Current standard:** Professional headshot, forward-facing, light background, confident expression. Not a selfie. Not a conference badge photo. Not a stock-looking corporate shot either.

**The standard:** Think "founder who's comfortable in front of a camera, not trying too hard." Shirt optional collar. Slight smile. The photo a journalist would use if they were profiling you.

### Banner Image
**What it should communicate:** Social proof + the core promise in one visual.

**Recommended content:**
- SELLL.io logo on the left
- One client outcome stat: "200+ daily conversations. 3–5% reply rate. 90 days."
- Or: Client logos (if clients have consented)
- Or: "Building Revenue Compounding Machines for B2B Teams"
- Background: dark brand color (not a generic LinkedIn template)

**What it must NOT be:** A generic "GTM Agency" banner with stock imagery of people shaking hands.

### Headline
**Current formula:** [What you build] + [For whom] + [Proof stat]

**Recommended headline:**
> Building Revenue Compounding Machines for B2B Teams | 200+ daily conversations, 3–5% reply rates | SELLL.io Founder

**Why this works:** When a CRO sees this after getting Aaron's email, it immediately confirms: (1) he does exactly what the email claimed, (2) the stats match, (3) he's the founder not a junior rep.

**What to avoid:**
- "CEO at SELLL.io" (too generic — what does SELLL do?)
- "AI-Powered GTM Expert" (meaningless buzz)
- "Helping companies grow revenue" (every agency says this)

### Engagement Rules (the algorithm matters)

**Within 60 minutes of posting:**
- Reply to every comment, even if it's just a one-liner
- The first 60 minutes determines whether LinkedIn pushes the post further
- A post with 5 genuine comments in the first hour gets 3x the organic reach of one with 0

**Engager detection (runs within 24–48h of every post):**
- Check who engaged with the post (likes + comments)
- Filter for ICP-fit profiles: title includes VP Sales / CRO / Founder / Head of Sales + company 25–150 employees
- ICP-fit engagers go into a warm outreach sequence (NOT the cold sequence)
- Warm DM template: reference the specific post + the specific insight they engaged with

**Warm DM from post engagement:**
```
[Name] — glad the [post topic] resonated.

[One sentence that extends the insight specifically to their situation based on their profile]

We're working on this exact problem with a few B2B teams right now.
Worth a quick DM conversation?

Aaron
```

**Target warm DM reply rate:** > 30% (vs. 5–10% for cold outreach)

## LinkedIn Signal Monitoring

Beyond posting, LinkedIn is the engine's most valuable signal source. Monitor these in real-time:

### 1. Who Viewed Your Profile
ICP-fit profile visitors = warm signals. If a VP Sales at a 50-person B2B SaaS company views the profile after receiving Email 2 — they're considering replying. Send a warm DM the same day.

**Protocol:**
```
[Name] — saw you checked out the profile. Happy to answer questions directly if it's easier.

[One line relevant to their situation based on their profile]

Worth a quick DM?

Aaron
```

### 2. Connection Requests from ICP
Incoming connection requests from VPs/CROs/Founders = warm inbound. Accept immediately + send a DM within 2 hours.

**First DM to inbound connection:**
```
[Name] — thanks for connecting. What's going on with your outbound motion at [Company] right now?

Aaron
```
Short. Direct. Opens a conversation without pitching.

### 3. Post Engagements from Target Accounts
If someone from a Tier 1 target account in the pipeline likes or comments on a post — that's a behavioral signal. Update their lead score (+10 behavioral). If they weren't in the pipeline yet, add them to the warm outreach queue.

### 4. Their Posts (Monitor Target Accounts)
For every Tier 1 account in the pipeline, monitor the LinkedIn activity of the primary contact. When they post about pipeline, GTM, or outbound challenges:
1. Comment substantively within 24 hours
2. Reference their post in the next email if they're in an active sequence
3. If post reveals new pain or new context, update their enrichment profile
