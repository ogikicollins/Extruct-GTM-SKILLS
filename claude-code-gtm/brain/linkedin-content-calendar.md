## Why This Calendar Exists

Every prospect who receives a LinkedIn follow from Aaron during pre-engagement will click on his profile. What they find in the first 3 seconds determines whether the follow is respected or ignored.

An empty profile → the follow means nothing.
A profile with 3 shallow posts → looks automated.
A profile with 12 posts showing real expertise over 4 weeks → the follow carries weight.

**Target:** By the time Email 1 lands in their inbox, the prospect has already seen Aaron's name on their LinkedIn notifications twice (T−3 follow + T−2 like) and has seen at least 4 posts demonstrating that this person knows their world.

---

## Content Principles

- **Never post about SELLL.io's services.** The feed is for insights, not ads.
- **One idea per post.** No listicles. No "here are 7 things I learned..."
- **Take a position.** The best posts divide opinion — some people agree strongly, some disagree. Both are good.
- **Short paragraphs.** No walls of text. Three lines maximum per paragraph.
- **No hashtag farming.** 0–2 hashtags maximum, only if genuinely relevant.
- **Real language.** No corporate speak. No "leverage," no "value-add," no "best practices."
- **End with a question or a sharp statement** — not "What do you think?"

## Week 1 — Establish Authority

### Post 1 (Monday) — The ICP Myth

**Hook:** `Most outbound teams think they have an ICP problem.`

```
Most outbound teams think they have an ICP problem.

They usually have a timing problem.

The ICP — company size, funding stage, vertical, headcount — is almost never what determines whether a cold email converts.

What determines it: did the right person receive the email at the exact moment they have the problem?

A 60-person SaaS company with a new VP Sales in week 3 and a Series A that closed last month is a completely different conversation than the same company six months later.

Same ICP. Completely different outcome.

The teams hitting 5%+ reply rates aren't smarter about who they target. They're more precise about when.
```

**Why this post:** Directly addresses the primary hypothesis (H5 timing-based targeting). ICP-definition is something every VP Sales and CRO has an opinion on. High engagement likelihood.

### Post 2 (Wednesday) — The Day 30 Reality

**Hook:** `What new VP Sales almost never say out loud in month one.`

```
What new VP Sales almost never say out loud in month one:

"The infrastructure I inherited was built for a company half our size."

They say it in month three, after the number has moved once.

In month one, most new VPs do the same thing: audit the team, pull the pipeline data, sit in on calls. They assume the playbook is approximately right and the execution needs work.

By month three they know the playbook was wrong.

The teams that figured this out by day 30 — not month three — are the ones that hit their 90-day targets.

Not always. But consistently.

**Why this post:** Speaks directly to the Persona 3 (New VP Sales) experience. Makes VPs who are in month one feel seen. Will drive engagement and DMs from the exact ICP.

### Post 3 (Friday) — Reply Rate Reality Check

**Hook:** `A 0.5% reply rate is not a content problem.`

```
A 0.5% reply rate is not a content problem.

Everyone thinks it is.

So they A/B test subject lines. They try shorter emails. They personalise the first line. They switch to video. They get to 0.7% and call it a win.

The problem is almost always upstream:
— The ICP hasn't been revisited in 12 months
— The signal triggering outreach has no urgency
— The sequence goes to 300 companies when 30 were the right fit

Better content into the wrong system produces better-written emails that still don't convert.

The teams hitting 5%+ started with the question: why would this specific person want to reply right now?

Not: how do we make our email more compelling?
```

**Why this post:** Directly contrarian — most outbound advice is about copy. This post says copy isn't the problem. Strong opinions generate comments. Will surface new connections in the VP Sales / RevOps space.

## Week 2 — Demonstrate Expertise

### Post 4 (Monday) — Volume vs. Conversion

**Hook:** `Most revenue leaders diagnose a volume problem when they have a conversion problem.`

```
Most revenue leaders diagnose a volume problem when they have a conversion problem.

Volume problem: your team can't generate enough conversations to hit pipeline target even at perfect conversion. Solution: more activity.

Conversion problem: conversations are happening. Qualified meetings aren't. Solution: ICP, signal selection, message architecture.

The two require completely different fixes.

The easy mistake: adding headcount when the targeting is off. You get more conversations with the wrong people. Costs $300K and three months to find out it didn't work.

I've watched companies make this mistake twice in two years — the second time with a harder internal conversation to have.

Diagnose before you build.
```

**Why this post:** Directly from CRO_v1 Email 2 framing. The Volume/Conversion diagnostic resonates with every revenue leader who's been through a missed quarter. Will generate engagement from CROs and Heads of Revenue.

### Post 5 (Wednesday) — The 45-Day Window

**Hook:** `The 45-day window most new VPs don't know they're in.`

```
The 45-day window most new VPs don't know they're in.

When a VP Sales joins a company, there's a brief period — usually 30 to 45 days — where rebuilding the outbound infrastructure is low-friction.

The team is expecting change. The board is patient. The rep's pipeline expectations haven't been set yet.

After day 45: change means disruption. The team has settled into the new normal. Reps don't want their targets moved. Leadership starts asking about early results.

The companies that used that window to rebuild from the intelligence layer up — ICP, signal, sequence — almost always hit their 90-day board targets.

The ones that waited to "learn the business first" started rebuilding in month three. Under different conditions.

One is easier. The other is more comfortable.
```

**Why this post:** The 45-day window is a core concept from the H5 hypothesis. Direct ICP magnet — any VP Sales who just joined a company will see this and feel the urgency. Expect DMs.

### Post 6 (Friday) — Founder-Led Sales Ceiling

**Hook:** `Most founders know exactly when they became the ceiling of their own company.`

```
Most founders know exactly when they became the ceiling of their own company.

It's not a dramatic moment. It's a quiet one.

A deal that only progressed because you were in the room. A quarter that ended well because you closed the last two yourself. A sales rep who's technically fine but who's producing half of what you do when you're involved.

The instinct is to keep being in the room. The number is better when you are.

The problem: the company can't grow past your bandwidth. And your bandwidth is already your most constrained resource.

The companies that break through this don't hire better salespeople. They build infrastructure that runs the pattern recognition you've spent years developing — and makes it repeatable without you in it.

That infrastructure is buildable. Most founders discover this too late.
```

**Why this post:** Directly from Founder_v1 Email 1 framing. "Most founders know exactly when they became the ceiling" is a line that every scaling founder recognises. Will be the highest-performing post in Week 2.

## Week 3 — Social Proof

### Post 7 (Monday) — The SDR Team Story (Devolon-style)

**Hook:** `4 SDRs. 35 conversations per day. The problem wasn't the team.`

```
4 SDRs. 35 conversations per day. The problem wasn't the team.

They were hitting their daily activity targets. The sequencer was running. The pipeline wasn't moving.

The assumption from leadership: the team needed more training, or the message needed work.

Neither was true.

The ICP was defined by company size and vertical — no signal layer. Sequences were going to companies that matched the profile but had no active urgency. The SDRs were reaching the right type of company at the wrong moment.

We rebuilt the targeting around a specific trigger: companies with a particular growth signal, at the moment that signal was freshest.

Same team. Same tools. 200+ conversations per day within 60 days.

The team wasn't the problem. What the team was pointed at was.
```

**Why this post:** Tells the Devolov story without naming them (appropriate for social). SDR Managers and RevOps leads in the ICP will share this with their VPs. The "same team, same tools" framing is a permission slip to not blame their team.

### Post 8 (Wednesday) — The Board Question

**Hook:** `The question that separates good VP Sales from great ones in a post-raise company.`

```
The question that separates good VP Sales from great ones in a post-raise company:

Not "what's the pipeline this month?"

"What does the pipeline look like in month three?"

Board reviews at Series A and B companies have a very specific shape. The first one after the raise is usually grace — you're still in setup mode. The second one is the test.

The VPs who prepare for the second review in the first 30 days — before the first one — almost always have something to show.

The ones who respond to the urgency of the first review without building for the second end up in a permanent reactive cycle.

The 90-day window is not about surviving month one. It's about having built something by month three that you can be held accountable to.
```

**Why this post:** H1+H5 compound scenario framing. Post-raise companies and their new VPs will engage strongly. Makes the board pressure feel like a solvable problem rather than an inevitable stressor.

### Post 9 (Friday) — What Makes Cold Email Work in 2026

**Hook:** `Cold email works. Just not the way most teams run it.`

```
Cold email works. Just not the way most teams run it.

The teams getting 5%+ reply rates aren't better writers. They're better at timing.

They reach the right person at the moment that person has the problem. Not a month before. Not a month after. At the exact window when the signal is fresh and the urgency is real.

A new VP Sales in week three is a completely different conversation than the same person in month six.

A company two months post-raise is a different conversation than the same company a year in.

The signal is the strategy. Everything else — the copy, the subject line, the sequence length — is execution.

Most teams optimise execution in a broken strategy.

Fix the strategy first.
```

**Why this post:** Summary of the entire SELLL positioning. Contrarian (cold email works). Explains signal-based targeting without pitching. Will attract engagement from both believers and skeptics — both are useful.

## Week 4 — Generate Leads

### Post 10 (Monday) — What the Best Motions Have in Common

**Hook:** `The outbound motions I've seen work best have one thing in common.`

```
The outbound motions I've seen work best have one thing in common.

They don't start with the sequence.

They start with a question: why would this specific person want to reply to this specific email right now — not in general, but today?

If you can't answer that question with specificity, the sequence doesn't matter.

The targeting answer is usually a signal: a new role, a raise, a job post, a competitor review, a LinkedIn post. Something that happened recently enough that the urgency is still real.

The message answer is: because the email demonstrates you understand the implication of that signal better than they've seen articulated.

Those two things together produce reply rates that look like outliers.

They're not. They're just better questions.
```

**Why this post:** Ties together the whole system into a simple summary. Drives engagement from VPs and CROs who are currently frustrated with their outbound results.

**Hook:** `The insight that changed how I think about outbound.`

```
The insight that changed how I think about outbound:

The prospect isn't ignoring your email because your subject line is wrong.

They're ignoring it because nothing about the email tells them you know what day 22 of a new VP Sales role feels like.

Or what it's like to inherit a motion that was built for a different stage. Or what it feels like to know the 90-day window is running and the infrastructure isn't there yet.

When the email shows you understand their exact situation with precision they haven't seen before — they reply. Not always. But at rates that change what the pipeline looks like.

The bar isn't "good email."

The bar is: does this person feel understood?

**Why this post:** The most personal, authentic post in the calendar. "Does this person feel understood?" is the entire SELLL philosophy in one line. Will drive DMs from exactly the right people.
