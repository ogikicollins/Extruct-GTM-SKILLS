## 3. Ideal Customer Profile

### ICP Summary
B2B company between $2M–$30M in revenue with an existing but underperforming sales team. They've bought the tools but haven't built a coherent system. SDRs spend 40–60% of their time on list-building instead of selling. Pipeline is unpredictable. Leadership is caught between "hire another SDR" and "fix the broken process first."

### Firmographic Sweet Spot

| Criteria | Ideal Range |
|----------|-------------|
| Annual Revenue | $2M–$30M ARR |
| Employees | 25–150 |
| Sales Team Size | 2–12 in sales roles |
| Company Stage | Series A, Series B, or bootstrapped $3M+ ARR |
| Geography | US (primary), UK, AU, CA (secondary) |
| YoY Growth Rate | 20–100% YoY |
| Vertical | B2B SaaS, Tech-Enabled Services, Fintech, HR Tech, Professional Services |

### Primary Verticals (ranked)
1. **B2B SaaS** — Recurring revenue, defined buying committee, ACV $5K–$100K
2. **Tech-Enabled Professional Services** — Complex sale, relationship-heavy, personalization has outsized impact
3. **Fintech / Insurtech** — High ACV, compliance-aware buyers who respond to credibility
4. **Sales & Marketing Tools** — Recursive ICP, high receptivity
5. **Business Intelligence / Analytics** — Data-driven buyers, ROI framing resonates

### Technographic Profile (Positive Signals)

| Category | Tools That Signal Fit |
|----------|-----------------------|
| CRM | HubSpot Pro/Enterprise, Salesforce Essentials/Pro |
| Prospecting | Apollo.io, ZoomInfo, Clay, Lusha |
| Sequencer | Outreach, Salesloft, Lemlist, Instantly |
| Ads | LinkedIn Campaign Manager, Meta Ads |
| LinkedIn | Sales Navigator (team license) |

### Technographic Red Flags (Do NOT pursue)
- Marketo or Pardot — enterprise marketing automation = internal team
- Outreach Enterprise with dedicated admin — over-built, will resist
- Custom in-house prospecting tools — "we'll build it ourselves" culture
- No CRM at all — too early

### ICP Scoring Rubric (100 points)
- Firmographic Fit: 25 pts
- Technographic Fit: 15 pts
- Pain Point Alignment: 20 pts
- Budget Capacity: 20 pts
- Contact Access: 10 pts
- Timing Signals: 10 pts

**Grade bands:** A+ (90–100) → 24h personalized outreach | A (75–89) → prioritize | B (60–74) → semi-personalized | C (40–59) → automated nurture | D (0–39) → do not pursue

### 60-Second Qualification Check
1. B2B company in SaaS, tech, or professional services? (Y/N)
2. 25–150 employees and $2M–$30M in revenue? (Y/N)
3. Growth signals: recent hire, funding, job posts? (Y/N)
4. Clear pain: list-building, low reply rates, unpredictable pipeline? (Y/N)
5. VP Sales, CRO, or CEO identifiable? (Y/N)

**5 Yes = likely A. 3–4 Yes = likely B. 1–2 Yes = C. 0 = D.**

## 5. Buyer Personas

### Persona 1: "The Pipeline-Pressured CRO"
**Title:** Chief Revenue Officer, VP Revenue, VP Sales (at company 2+ years)
**Age:** 36–48
**Reports to:** CEO
**KPIs:** Quarterly pipeline generated, revenue attainment, CAC, pipeline coverage ratio (3x+)

**Pain language:**
- "We have all the tools but the team still can't generate consistent pipeline."
- "I'm building the plane while flying it — nobody gave me a playbook."
- "My SDRs are burning out and I don't know if it's the message, the list, or the channel."

**Best triggers:** Series A/B funding in last 6 months OR SDR team exists but pipeline is inconsistent
**Information diet:** 30 Minutes to President's Club, Scott Barker GTM Newsletter, Pavilion Slack, Kyle Coleman, Jason Lemkin on LinkedIn
**Wins on:** Devolon case study (35 → 200+ daily activities) | ICP audit as low-risk first step

**Key objections:**
- "We've tried agencies before" → Counter: Show the intelligence phase is diagnostic-first, not templated
- "How is this different from Apollo?" → Counter: SELLL is an orchestration layer; Apollo is one of 45+ data sources
- "We need to fix positioning first" → Counter: The intelligence phase IS how you fix positioning

---

### Persona 2: "The Scaling Founder Still Closing Deals"
**Title:** CEO / Co-Founder ($3M–$10M ARR, 20–80 employees)
**Age:** 28–42
**Situation:** Built $0 to $3M–$8M on founder-led sales and referrals. Has 1–3 AEs but still closes the biggest deals personally.

**Pain language:**
- "I've hired salespeople but I'm still the one closing everything."
- "We've tried cold email but it doesn't work like it used to."
- "I don't have time to build a sales system AND run the company."

**Best triggers:** Company hit $3M+ ARR, no VP Sales yet, founder still posting about deals on LinkedIn
**Information diet:** Lenny's Newsletter, SaaStr, MicroConf, peer founder Slack groups (OnDeck, YC alumni)
**Wins on:** Flow Meditation case study (founder removed from sales) | 90-day end state (they OWN the system, not a forever retainer)

**Key objections:**
- "Only I can sell this" → Counter: We encode your voice into the system, not replace it
- "We're not ready" → Counter: If ACV is $10K+ and you're doing founder-led sales, you're ready
- "I hate being locked in" → Counter: 90-day defined end state, they own the system after

---

### Persona 3: "The VP Sales Inheriting a Broken Stack"
**Title:** VP of Sales, Head of Sales (in role < 90 days)
**Age:** 32–44
**Situation:** Joined from a more mature GTM company. Inherited chaos: inconsistent tool usage, no playbook, sequences with 8% open rates.

**Pain language:**
- "I inherited a stack but not a system."
- "Every SDR is doing something different and none of it is working."
- "I need to show results in 90 days before the board loses confidence."

**Best triggers:** LinkedIn shows new role start date within last 60 days
**Wins on:** Holz Concepts case study (Stefan Golz hit first pipeline targets on time) | Position SELLL as the execution partner for VP's transformation plan — they diagnosed the problem, SELLL implements it

**Key objections:**
- "Too early to bring in outside help" → Counter: Intelligence phase replaces weeks of internal discovery with validated answer in 3 weeks
- "CFO needs to approve" → Counter: Help them build the business case; 90-day build with defined deliverable is easier to approve than open-ended retainer
- "We tried an outbound agency last year" → Counter: Ask what broke (list, message, or follow-through); address specifically
