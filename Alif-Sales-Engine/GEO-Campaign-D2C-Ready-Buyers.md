# Alif GEO Campaign — D2C Solo-Founder Ready Buyers

**What this is:** A net-new cold outbound campaign for Alif to sell GEO
(Generative Engine Optimization) as its own service line. Distinct from
[GEO-Warm-Lead-Sequence.md](GEO-Warm-Lead-Sequence.md), which is for leads
who already opted in — this campaign targets prospects who have never
heard from Alif before.

---

## 1. ICP: D2C Solo-Founder "Ready Buyers"

**Who:** Solo-founder-led D2C brands where "best X for Y" is a real
purchase-decision query pattern — skincare, supplements, home goods, pet
products, apparel/accessories, gadgets, wellness, food & bev. One person
owns the growth/marketing budget decision; there's no marketing team
standing between the founder and a reply.

**Firmographics:**
- Revenue: $300K–$5M/yr — big enough to have real budget, small enough that the founder still personally owns growth spend
- Team size: 1–10, founder is the final decision-maker on marketing
- Motion: DTC e-commerce (Shopify or similar) or a digital product/subscription
- Already spending on paid ads, SEO/content, or influencer — proves acquisition budget exists and is actively deployed

**"Ready buyer" signals** (what elevates a prospect above the general D2C population — check before sending):
1. **Category is provably AI-search-relevant.** Running "best {{category}} for {{use case}}" through ChatGPT surfaces named competitors, not them. This is the qualifier *and* the personalization hook in one motion.
2. Recent public signal of growth pain — a podcast/interview/X thread mentioning declining organic traffic, rising CAC, or a new growth hire.
3. Founder is publicly vocal (X/LinkedIn, founder-led brand story) — they'll personally read and reply to outbound.
4. Prior/current use of SEO tooling (Ahrefs, Semrush, Clearscope) — shows search literacy, so the GEO concept lands without a long education arc.
5. AOV/LTV high enough to justify a retainer — skip sub-$20 impulse categories with thin margins.

**Disqualifiers:** VC-backed with a 5+ person marketing team (decision isn't the founder's alone); pure commodity/price-driven category with no "recommendation" purchase pattern; founder with no public presence to verify signals against.

**Where to find them:** D2C founder communities on X, Shopify App Store reviews / public revenue signals, Product Hunt, D2C-focused newsletter sponsor lists, Ahrefs/Semrush traffic-decline reports cross-referenced against Shopify domains.

## 2. Pre-send qualification (do this before touch 1, per prospect)

1. Identify the 1–3 "best {{category}} for {{use case}}" queries a buyer in their space would actually ask ChatGPT.
2. Run the query live. Note which brands get cited.
3. Confirm the prospect's brand is absent. If they already show up, they're not a fit for this campaign — route to a different offer.
4. Note the cited brands — touch 1 uses the count ("three brands showed up") and touch 3 references it again for continuity.

This is what makes the cold open land: it's not a generic pain claim, it's a specific, checkable observation about their exact business.

## 3. Cold Sequence (3 touches, <80 words each)

**Fill in before sending:**
- `{{First Name}}`, `{{Brand}}`
- `{{category}}` / `{{use case}}` — the exact ChatGPT query run in step 1 above
- `{{Time 1}}` / `{{Time 2}}` — two concrete open slots

### Touch 1 — Day 0
**Subject:** Ran {{Brand}} through ChatGPT this morning

{{First Name}} — quick one. Asked ChatGPT "best {{category}} for {{use case}}" this morning. Three brands showed up. {{Brand}} wasn't one of them.

That's not an ads or SEO problem — it's a GEO problem: ChatGPT cites brands with clear entity data and answer-ready content, and skips everyone else.

Worth 15 minutes to show you exactly where the gap is? {{Time 1}} or {{Time 2}}.

*(59 words)*

### Touch 2 — Day 2–3
**Subject:** The data behind why this matters

{{First Name}}, following up with the why.

Brands that fix entity clarity, answer-first content, and third-party authority see up to 40% more visibility in AI-generated answers (Princeton GEO research). For D2C, that's the difference between being the "recommended" pick and never entering the conversation.

Want a free snapshot of where {{Brand}} shows up (or doesn't) across your category's top ChatGPT queries? Reply "send it."

*(63 words)*

### Touch 3 — Day 5–6
**Subject:** Last one — closing the loop

{{First Name}}, last note from me.

Every week {{Brand}} isn't the cited answer, one of those three brands becomes the default recommendation in your category instead — and once buyers trust a pick, they stop comparing.

Skip the snapshot if you'd rather just talk — 15 minutes, I'll show you the gap and what closes it.

{{Time 1}} or {{Time 2}}?

*(57 words)*

⚠️ Same caveat as the warm sequence: the "up to 40%" figure is a
third-party research citation (Princeton GEO study), not an Alif client
result — verify before send, swap for a real case study once Alif has one.

## 4. Why this converts
- **The trigger is provable per-prospect**, not a generic claim — a founder can go verify the ChatGPT query themselves in 10 seconds, which is what makes touch 1 land instead of reading as a template.
- **Founder-to-founder tone** — short, direct, no corporate throat-clearing — matches how a solo founder actually reads their inbox.
- **"Ready buyer" pre-qualification does the targeting work** so the cold sequence can go straight to a meeting ask in touch 1 and 3, same motion that's already converting in the warm sequence, instead of needing a softer multi-step cold ramp.
- **Continuity detail** ("three brands," referenced again in touch 3) signals this was actually researched, not mail-merged.
